# Playtest -- 2026-03-08 07:03:06

Seed: 42 | Ticks played: ~161 (VICTORY)

## The Experience

I won the game at tick 161 with 112.4K dead. The rebalanced game is genuinely winnable with strategic play. The session felt like a race against time: identify diseases, develop medicines, deploy aggressively, and mop up the stragglers before they spread to new regions. The pacing was tight -- at ~80 seconds of real time, this is a fast, satisfying arc from "what is happening" to "humanity is saved."

Starting state: 50.5K infected (50K Strain Alpha in Asia, 500 Strain Beta in South America). I had to identify both pathogens (unknown at start), develop medicines, and deploy them before the diseases spiraled.

Key moments:
- Treated 100K in Asia with untested Antiviral-A, got an ADVERSE REACTION that killed 20K people. Brutal but necessary.
- Second deployment to Asia (86.1K treated) had no adverse reaction -- lucky roll.
- Broad-Spectrum Antiviral with 500K doses was overkill for 19.1K infected in South America, but wiped it out completely.
- Final 3 infected in Europe cleaned up with Antiviral-A for $200.
- Victory triggered at tick 161 with 0 infected worldwide.

## What Stuck With Me

**The rebalance works.** Medicine doses (100K for Antiviral-A, 500K for Broad-Spectrum) are now meaningful against infection counts in the tens-to-hundreds of thousands. Two deployments of Antiviral-A cleared 186K infected in Asia. This is a massive improvement from the previous 10K-50K doses that were reported as useless.

**Knowledge gating on the threats panel works correctly.** At 0% knowledge, both pathogens showed "Unknown Pathogen #1/2" with "???" and no per-region data. At 34%, names appeared with partial stats (Infect rate visible, Lethal/Recovery shown as "?"). At 68%, all stats were revealed. Per-region infection tables only appeared once knowledge crossed the threshold.

**Zero-target deployment feedback works.** When I tried to treat 0 infected in North America, the status bar showed "No infected population in North America" and no funds were deducted.

**The untested medicine warning is clear and impactful.** The warning screen with "25% chance of adverse effects -- 20% of doses will KILL instead of help" and the "Deploy anyway?" prompt felt appropriately scary. When the adverse reaction triggered, the status bar clearly showed "ADVERSE REACTION: 20.0K died."

**Research progress in the header works well.** "Field: Identifying Strain Beta 0% | Bench: Antiviral-A 50%" gives clear at-a-glance status without opening any panel.

**Victory condition may be too lenient.** The win condition says "all diseases identified" but I won with Strain Beta at only 68% knowledge (two ID rounds, not three). The game only checked for 0 infected. Either the description is wrong or the check is wrong.

## What I Wanted To Do But Couldn't

**Test insufficient funds error.** I never actually ran out of money in a way that let me test the error message. By the time I was deploying, I always had enough funds. The passive income ($5/tick) accumulates fast when you spend the early game on research. I would have needed to spend more aggressively to hit this edge case.

**Navigate the multi-key sequences more reliably.** Several times my enter-enter-enter chains to navigate research menus closed the panel instead of drilling in. The issue is that pressing 'r' opens research, but also toggles it -- so if a stray 'r' key fires while the panel is open, it closes. I had to be very deliberate about step-by-step navigation. In snapshot mode this is manageable, but I can imagine it being frustrating in real-time.

**See a summary or score screen at victory.** The victory state shows "All diseases eradicated! Humanity is saved." in the status bar, but there is no score breakdown -- no time taken, deaths, money spent, medicines deployed. For a game you just won, you want a moment of satisfaction with some stats.

**Vaccinate proactively.** The vaccinate-vs-treat decision exists but there is no real strategic tension. Treating infected is almost always the right call because you need to get to 0 infected to win. Vaccination only matters for prevention, but the diseases spread slowly enough that you can just treat reactively. With 500K doses of BSA used to vaccinate South America when I meant to treat, I accidentally wasted $500 -- and even then it did not matter because I still had enough money.

## Ideas

1. **Victory screen with stats.** Show total ticks, total dead, total money spent, medicines deployed, adverse reactions triggered. Give the player a sense of how well they did, not just that they won.

2. **Fix the win condition knowledge check.** If the intended requirement is "all diseases identified," enforce it. With Strain Beta at 68% knowledge, I had not fully identified it, yet I still won. Either lower the threshold or enforce it properly.

3. **Insufficient funds test path.** Consider starting with less money or making medicines more expensive so players actually encounter the funds error during normal play. With $5/tick passive income and $200-500 medicine costs, you accumulate faster than you spend.

4. **Deployment result summary.** When deploying 500K doses to treat 19.1K infected, the status bar says "Treated 19.1K" which is correct. But it would be nice to know how many doses were wasted (unused). 500K doses for 19.1K infected means 480.9K doses went unused -- that is information the player should see.

5. **Multi-disease deploy target list is confusing.** When Broad-Spectrum Antiviral shows the region list, each region has two sets of numbers (one per disease) jammed onto one line: "South America 430.0Ms 0i 429.5Ms 19.1Ki 512.1K". This is very hard to parse. Label which disease each set of numbers belongs to.
