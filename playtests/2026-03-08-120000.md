# Playtest -- 2026-03-08 12:00

Seed: 4242 (primary), 7777 (verification) | Ticks played: ~700

## The Experience

The map is a clear upgrade from a flat table. Opening the game, I immediately see six region boxes arranged in a 3x2 grid: NA/EU/Asia on top, SA/Africa/Oceania on the bottom. Connection lines (horizontal dashes, vertical pipes, diagonal slashes) tie them together and give a sense of geography. Selecting a region shows per-disease infection detail inside the box, which is a natural way to drill in without opening a separate panel.

I spent most of my time navigating the map and watching disease spread. Arrow keys moved selection cleanly between regions -- up/down switched rows, left/right switched columns. Boundary behavior was solid: pressing left at NA just stayed put, no crashes or wrapping. The NA-Oceania connection hint ("North America" below Oceania, "Oceania" below NA) was a nice touch for the wrap-around connection that can't be drawn with a line.

By tick 200, disease had spread to all regions. Strain Alpha was dominant in Asia (16.6M infected), while Strain Beta had taken hold in South America (3.2M). The threat levels (OK/LOW/MOD/HIGH/CRIT) updated correctly as infections grew. By tick 700, half a billion were dead and almost everything was CRIT.

## What Stuck With Me

**The map layout works well.** The 3x2 grid is immediately readable. Connection lines between regions give a sense of disease pathways. The threat level badge (OK/LOW/MOD/HIGH/CRIT) in the corner of each box is useful at a glance.

**Health bars feel invisible.** Every health bar looked identical (all solid cyan blocks) across the entire 700-tick session. Even with 500M dead out of 7.8B total population, that's only ~6% of bars. The segments for infected/dead/immune are calculated proportionally against total regional population, so they'd need to represent a very large share of a region before becoming visible in a 28-character-wide bar. The health bar never once gave me useful information that wasn't already in the text.

**Per-disease detail in the selected box is great.** Clicking into Asia and seeing "Strain Alpha: 16.6M inf" and "Strain Beta: 82 inf" felt like the right amount of information. "No infections" for clean regions was clear.

**Half the screen is empty.** The map occupies roughly the top third of the screen. The entire bottom half is blank space inside the World Map border. This feels wasteful -- either the map boxes could be taller to show more data, or the outer box should hug the content.

**Split view with panels works.** Opening Threats or Medicines alongside the map shrank the map to the left half but kept everything readable. The region boxes maintained their shape and the connection lines still rendered correctly.

## What I Wanted To Do But Couldn't

**I wanted to click on a region to select it.** Arrow key navigation works, but with a visual map, pointing feels natural.

**I wanted the health bar to tell me something.** I kept glancing at the bars hoping to see red or gray segments appear as things got worse, but they never changed visually. The bar is technically correct but practically useless at the current scale. A region-relative view (showing only the affected population, not the entire population) would make the bar useful much sooner.

**I wanted to see population numbers change.** The population line always says "Pop: 500.0M" for North America even after millions die. It would be useful to see the living population decrease, or show a delta.

**I wanted the map to use its empty space.** There's room for per-region summaries, mini charts, or at least larger boxes that could show more disease detail lines.

## Ideas

1. **Make health bars region-relative.** Instead of proportioning against total population (where 1M infected in 4.7B is invisible), consider a logarithmic scale, or show the bar relative to the affected population only. Even simpler: when infections are tiny relative to population, show a minimum 1-character red segment so the bar provides at least some visual signal.

2. **Tighten the World Map bounding box.** Either shrink it to hug the map content (two rows of boxes + gap), or use the extra vertical space to make region boxes taller and show more per-region information (multiple disease lines, trend indicators, etc).

3. **Show population changes.** Display "Pop: 498.2M (-1.8M)" or turn the population number red when deaths are significant.

4. **Consider minimum visible segments.** If infected > 0, guarantee at least 1 character of red in the health bar. If dead > 0, guarantee at least 1 character of gray. This makes the bar useful as a boolean indicator even when proportions are tiny.
