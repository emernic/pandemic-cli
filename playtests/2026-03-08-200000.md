# Playtest -- 2026-03-08 20:00

Seed: 42 | Ticks played: ~985

## The Experience

I approached this as a strategic optimizer trying to beat the game as efficiently as possible. The full pipeline works: identify threats, develop medicine, run clinical trial, deploy. But the game has a fundamental balance problem -- medicine is irrelevant at the scale the diseases operate.

I played optimally: started research at the earliest possible moment (tick 10, as soon as I had 10 RP), ran field and bench research in parallel when possible, completed the full Antiviral-A pipeline by tick 135. I deployed medicine twice to Asia. Then I stopped deploying because 10K doses against 91.8M infected is 0.01% -- literally meaningless. I watched 1.3 billion people die over ~985 ticks while sitting on $5,425 and 890 RP with nothing useful to spend them on.

The experience felt like watching a nature documentary about a wildfire while holding a squirt gun.

## What Stuck With Me

**The research pipeline is well-designed.** The flow from identify -> develop -> trial -> deploy is intuitive and has real decisions (which disease to prioritize, whether to skip the clinical trial). Running field + bench research in parallel felt like a genuine strategic lever. The UI for navigating research categories, confirming projects, and seeing progress is clean.

**Medicine doses are absurdly undersized.** Antiviral-A: 10K doses, $250. Strain Alpha in Asia: 91.8M infected at tick 285. That is a 9,180:1 ratio of infected-to-doses. Even if I spent every dollar deploying medicine, I would treat ~80K people while millions more get infected per tick. The Broad-Spectrum Antiviral (50K doses) is only 5x better -- still negligible.

**Resources pile up with nothing to spend them on.** By tick 985 I had $5,425 and 890 RP. Funding income is $5/tick, RP is +1/tick. After finishing research (which costs ~85 RP total for the full Alpha pipeline), RP has no use. Funding could theoretically buy medicine deployments, but each deployment treats so few people it does not matter.

**No win or lose condition.** The game just... keeps going. Diseases burn through populations, natural immunity builds, infections decline, but there is no endgame. No score, no "humanity survived" message, no failure state. At 1.3B dead I felt nothing because the game gave me no frame for whether that was good or bad.

**Three identification rounds feels tedious.** Each identify adds 0.34 knowledge. You need 0.75 for medicine development. That means 3 rounds minimum (0 -> 0.34 -> 0.68 -> 1.0). Each is 20 ticks and 10 RP. The second and third rounds feel like busy-work -- no new information, same button, same wait. The first identification is exciting (you learn the disease name and eventually its stats). The next two are just clicking the same thing again.

**Untested medicine risk is invisible.** I deployed Antiviral-A twice while it was [UNTESTED]. The 25% adverse effects chance should be dramatic -- 20% of doses killing instead of curing. But there was zero feedback. No message saying "adverse reaction killed X people." No visible consequence. I could not tell if the adverse effect triggered or not.

**Disease-to-medicine mapping is unclear.** The bench research shows "Develop: Antiviral-A" and "Develop: Broad-Spectrum Antiviral" but does not say which diseases they target. I had to deploy Antiviral-A to see "Strain Alpha" in the deployment screen. This information should be on the research confirmation screen.

## What I Wanted To Do But Couldn't

**Mass-deploy medicine.** I wanted to spend $2000 deploying 8 rounds of Antiviral-A in one action, not navigate through 8 rounds of region selection. Even better: a "deploy all available doses to highest-need region" button.

**See remaining susceptible population.** The threats panel shows infected, immune, and dead. But the key strategic number is susceptible (total - infected - immune - dead). That tells you how much runway the disease has. I had to do mental math with 4.7B minus three large numbers.

**Prioritize research on the bigger threat.** Both diseases cost the same to research. Strain Alpha (15% infect, 2% lethal) is clearly more dangerous than Strain Beta (8% infect, 0.5% lethal). But there is no way to fast-track research on the more urgent one. No "emergency research" option that costs more but finishes faster.

**See research progress while in other panels.** Once I started research, I wanted to keep an eye on the progress bar while checking threats or deploying medicine. The research progress is only visible inside the research panel.

**Know whether I was winning or losing.** No score, no projection, no "at this rate X will die by tick Y." The numbers just go up. I had no frame of reference for whether my actions mattered.

## Ideas

1. **Scale medicine doses to match disease scale.** 10K doses is appropriate for a regional outbreak of thousands. For a pandemic of millions, you need millions of doses. Either scale doses up (100K-1M per deployment), make doses scale with research investment, or let the player manufacture more doses over time.

2. **Add a score or objective.** "Keep deaths below X" or "eradicate all diseases" or even just a running score based on lives saved. The player needs a goal to optimize toward.

3. **Collapse repeat identification into a single longer project.** Instead of 3x "Identify: Strain Alpha" at 20 ticks each, make it one "Deep Analysis: Strain Alpha" at 40 ticks that goes from 34% to 100%. Or make each round reveal new information (round 2 reveals transmission mode, round 3 reveals vulnerabilities) so there is a reason to care about each step.

4. **Add feedback for adverse medicine effects.** When untested medicine causes harm, show a message: "Adverse reaction: 2,000 of 10,000 doses caused fatalities." Make it visible and consequential so the clinical trial decision feels meaningful.

5. **Show disease-medicine targeting in research UI.** The bench research confirmation screen should say "Develop: Antiviral-A (targets Strain Alpha)" so the player can make informed decisions.

6. **Add resource sinks.** Excess funding and RP need uses: upgrade medicine production (more doses per deployment), build field hospitals (reduce lethality in a region), fund public health campaigns (reduce infection rate), emergency quarantine measures. The player should never be sitting on 890 unused RP.

7. **Add a status bar or ticker showing research progress.** Something like "Field: Identify Strain Beta 45% | Bench: Idle" in the hotkey bar area, so you always know what is running.
