# Playtest -- 2026-03-08 15:01

Seed: 7777 | Ticks played: ~490

## The Experience

I started with two unknown pathogens -- one big outbreak in Asia (50K infected) and a small one in South America (500). The early game had a clear cadence: identify the big threat, develop a broad-spectrum medicine, deploy it. By tick 110, I had knocked Asia from 145K infected down to zero with two untested Broad-Spectrum deployments. South America's 6.2K infections from Pathogen #2 were wiped in a third deployment. Total cost: $1,200.

Then came the long middle game. From tick 140 to 440 (300 ticks, about 2.5 real-time minutes), infections slowly grew back from single-digit seedings across 4 regions, eventually reaching 8K total. Resources piled up ($2,000, 345 RP) with nothing meaningful to spend them on. I ran a clinical trial and developed Antiviral-A mostly for completeness.

The interesting moment came at tick 440 when I went to re-deploy Broad-Spectrum: the diseases had mutated. "Strain outdated! (50% strain match)" meant my tested Broad-Spectrum was now only half as effective. That was a genuine surprise -- the game had quietly evolved the threat while I was coasting. I treated N. America anyway (38% efficacy due to partial mutation) and saw infections stubbornly growing in Europe by tick 490.

## What Stuck With Me

**The mutation/strain drift system is the standout feature.** Without it, the game would be "research -> deploy -> win." With it, there is a real ongoing threat. Discovering at tick 440 that my carefully trialed medicine was now only 50% effective against Strain Alpha was a genuinely interesting moment. It creates a reason to keep spending RP and personnel.

**The untested medicine warning is well-designed.** The confirmation dialog clearly states the risk (25% chance adverse effects, 20% of doses kill). In a crisis with 145K infected in Asia, deploying untested felt like a real gamble. The "(50% efficacy)" feedback on deployment made the penalty tangible.

**Research flow is clean.** Identify -> Develop -> Clinical Trial -> Deploy is intuitive. The UI for navigating this (Field Research / Bench Research categories, confirmation screens, [ACTIVE] labels, progress percentage in the header) all works well. Running field and bench in parallel feels natural.

**The deploy-to-region-then-pick-target flow works.** Being able to choose between vaccinate susceptible and treat infected per disease per region gives granular control. Returning to the region list after deployment enables rapid multi-region treatment.

**The early game has urgency, the mid-game does not.** Ticks 0-110 felt tense: Asia was escalating fast, I was racing to identify and develop. Ticks 110-440 felt like waiting. Cross-region spread from single-digit seedings is too slow to create pressure.

## What I Wanted To Do But Couldn't

**Check active research progress without re-opening the panel.** The header shows "Field: Trial: Broad-Spectrum 0%" which is good, but I wanted to see a tick countdown or a more prominent progress bar. In the long idle stretch, I kept wondering "how many ticks until my trial finishes?"

**Prioritize which regions to protect.** I wanted to triage -- "Europe has 750M people, protect it first" -- but there is no way to set priorities or queue deployments. Each deploy is manual.

**Understand disease stats before full identification.** At tick 10, I had 50K infected in Asia and no idea what the lethality was. I would have liked partial intel from the map -- even just "lethality: HIGH" based on the death rate I could observe. The game has the data (I can see deaths on the map), but the Threats panel shows "???" until knowledge reaches the threshold.

**See a global timeline or graph.** After 490 ticks, I wanted to see the arc of the outbreak -- the surge, my interventions, the recovery. A sparkline or simple history would make the narrative of the game visible.

## Ideas

**Strain drift should force harder choices about medicine strategy.** Right now Antiviral-A ($200, narrow) and Broad-Spectrum ($400, broad) cost the same RP and time to develop. The broad-spectrum is strictly better since it covers both diseases. What if narrow antivirals resisted strain drift longer (say, 500 ticks before efficacy drops) while broad-spectrum drifted faster (200 ticks)? Then you have a real trade-off: develop the cheap narrow antiviral that stays effective longer for the immediate crisis, or invest in the expensive broad-spectrum that covers everything but needs constant re-trialing. You could even have a research option to "update" a medicine to the current strain (cheaper than a full re-trial), creating a maintenance treadmill.

**Cross-region spread needs transmission events, not just a slow drip.** Right now spread seeds at 1 infected per tick per connection, which is barely noticeable. What if instead, there were discrete "transmission events" -- a cargo ship docks in Europe carrying Strain Alpha, seeding 500 infected at once? These could appear as alerts: "ALERT: Strain Alpha detected in Europe -- 500 cases from shipping route." The player could then choose to deploy medicine to that region before it spirals. This would create punctuated moments of decision rather than a slow background drip. Travel restrictions (the Policy panel) could reduce the frequency of these events at an economic cost.

**Vaccination should create visible herd immunity progress.** When I vaccinate 100K people in a region of 500M, it feels like nothing (0.02% of the population). The game could show an "immunity %" per region per disease, and once it crosses a threshold (say 60%), the disease stops spreading there. Now vaccination is a long-term investment with a visible finish line. You would have to decide: do I vaccinate Asia's 4.7B people (expensive, slow) or concentrate on protecting Oceania's 45M (cheap, fast)? Small regions become strategic strongholds.

**The idle mid-game needs resource drains.** Between tick 140 and 440, I had nothing to spend money or RP on. What if maintaining supply chains for medicines cost ongoing funds? What if personnel needed to be "stationed" in regions to enable fast deployment (otherwise there is a 10-tick delay before medicine takes effect)? What if disease surveillance (knowing infection counts at all) required funded monitoring stations, and without them you only see "UNKNOWN" for infection counts in unmonitored regions? Suddenly you are choosing between visibility, readiness, and research.

**The defeat path is too distant to feel real.** 10% of global population is ~760M dead. At tick 490 I had 71.7K dead total. Even at the worst moment (Asia with 145K infected and maybe 2% lethality per tick), the death rate was maybe 3K/tick. It would take thousands of ticks to approach defeat. The threat level thresholds (LOW/MOD/HIGH/CRIT) are region-level alerts, but there is no global "danger meter" showing how close you are to the 10% threshold. A visible doomsday clock would add tension even in the slow periods.

**Tick 110:** Asia cleared to 0 infected. S. America 6.2K from Pathogen #2. Treated S. America too. Total infections down to 22 globally.

**Tick 140:** All major outbreaks suppressed. Tiny seedings remain (5-16 per region). $500, 90 RP.

**Tick 190:** Slow growth continues. 50 total infected. Started clinical trial for Broad-Spectrum vs Strain Alpha (15 RP, 5 personnel, 25 ticks). Header shows "Field: Trial: Broad-Spectrum 0%". Also started Antiviral-A development on bench.

**Tick 240:** Both research projects done. Medicines panel shows Broad-Spectrum [Tested: 1/2], Antiviral-A [UNTESTED]. Bench shows "No projects available" -- everything developable is developed. 137 infected, resources piling up (145 RP).

**Tick 440:** Advanced 200 ticks. Infections grew back to 8.1K total. N. America 4K (mostly Pathogen #2), Europe 2.6K. $2000 and 345 RP accumulated. Tried to deploy Broad-Spectrum and discovered strain drift: "Strain outdated! (50% strain match -- re-trial to update)." Effective doses dropped dramatically. This was the most interesting discovery of the session.

**Tick 490:** Treated N. America at 38% efficacy. Europe growing to 6.9K. Game is stable but not won -- strain drift means the disease will keep coming back unless I continuously re-trial. Final state: $1850, 395 RP, 10K infected, 71.7K dead.
