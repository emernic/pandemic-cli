# Playtest -- 2026-03-08 08:33

Seed: 77 | Ticks played: ~415 actively, ran to defeat at tick 1760

## The Experience

Started fresh with seed 77. Two unknown pathogens, 50.5K infected across Asia and South America. Spent the first 80 ticks identifying threats through field research, then pivoted to bench research to develop medicines. By tick 170 I had Antiviral-A unlocked and deployed 100K doses to treat Asia's 381K Strain Alpha infected. The treatment made a visible dent but exponential growth quickly outpaced it. By tick 335 I had BSA developed but was already losing badly -- 3.1M infected, 1.5M dead. Ran to defeat at tick 1760 with 785M dead (10% of world population).

The game felt like a race I was always behind in. The research pipeline is satisfyingly deep (identify twice, develop medicine, optionally run clinical trial) but the time pressure is real -- 40 ticks sitting in bench research while millions get infected creates genuine tension.

## What Stuck With Me

**Misleading zero-target feedback message.** When deploying BSA to treat Strain Beta in Asia (where Strain Beta has 0 infected but Strain Alpha has 2M), the status bar says "No infected population in Asia." This is factually wrong -- there ARE infected people in Asia, just not with the targeted strain. The message should say something like "No Strain Beta infected in Asia" or "No targets for this treatment." A player could easily misread this as a bug.

**Ghost disease entries after failed deployment.** When I attempted to treat Strain Alpha in South America (0 infected), the deployment was correctly blocked, but a disease_state entry for Strain Alpha was created in South America with all zeros. This caused "Strain Alpha" to appear on the South America region card for the rest of the game, even though Strain Alpha never actually reached South America. This is a cosmetic data bug -- failed deployments should not create disease tracking entries.

**Research progress header truncation.** When identifying "Unknown Pathogen #2", the progress header showed "Field: Identifying Unknown 0%" -- the name is truncated to just "Unknown" instead of "Unknown Pathogen #2". Minor but noticeable.

**Knowledge display shows 100% after 2 identifications.** Each identification adds 0.34 knowledge (per design docs), so 2 rounds should give 0.68. But the threats panel showed "Knowledge: 100%". Either the display rounds up aggressively, or the actual knowledge value exceeds what the docs say. The raw save confirmed knowledge=1.0 for Strain Alpha after 2 identifications, so either each round adds 0.5 (not 0.34) or there's a different calculation path.

**Interactive mode error message is unhelpful.** Running without `--snapshot` in a non-TTY environment produces `Error: Os { code: 25, kind: Uncategorized, message: "Inappropriate ioctl for device" }`. This is a raw Rust OS error. Should catch this and say "Interactive mode requires a terminal. Use --snapshot for non-interactive usage."

## What I Wanted To Do But Couldn't

**No way to see total population breakdown per region.** The threats panel shows infected/immune/dead per disease per region, but there's no single view that shows susceptible population. During deployment I could see "4.7B pop" and "381K inf" but not how many are immune or susceptible. I wanted a region detail view.

**No notification when research completes.** Research finishes silently. If you're not watching the progress header, you miss it. A status bar message like "Research complete: Antiviral-A developed!" would help.

**Cannot queue research.** When field research finishes, I have to manually navigate back to start the next project. Would be nice to queue the next one.

## Ideas

1. **Fix the zero-target feedback message** to include the disease name: "No Strain Beta infected in Asia" instead of "No infected population in Asia." This is the highest-priority fix -- it's actively misleading.

2. **Prevent ghost disease entries** from failed deployments. The zero-target check should bail out before creating a RegionInfection entry.

3. **Add research completion notifications** to the status bar. Even a one-tick message would help.

4. **Improve the interactive mode error** to suggest `--snapshot` when no TTY is detected.

5. **Consider showing the disease name** in the research progress header without truncation, or use a shorter format like "Field: Identifying #2 25%".
