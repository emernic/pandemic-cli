# Playtest -- 2026-03-08 10:33:10

Seed: 777, 888, 999 | Ticks played: ~500 across 3 games (221 to victory, ~1811 to defeat, ~110 partial)

## The Experience

I played three distinct sessions with different strategic approaches. Game 777 was the "wrong strategy first" run where I deliberately identified the minor threat (Strain Beta, 500 infected in South America) before the major one (Strain Alpha, 50K infected in Asia). Despite the suboptimal start, I managed to win at tick 221 with 66.5K total dead. Game 888 was a "do nothing" run to test the defeat path -- I started a single identify project at tick 200, then let the world burn until defeat at tick 1811 with 784.8M dead (exactly 10.0% of population). Game 999 tested the research pipeline requirements and revealed that BSA development has strict knowledge prerequisites.

The game feels complete and polished. The research-to-deployment pipeline has real strategic depth, and the "wrong choices" experience is well-handled -- wasting 40 ticks identifying the minor threat while Asia burns from 4.3K to 27.5K dead creates genuine tension.

## What Stuck With Me

**Untested medicine warning shown before funds check.** When trying to deploy BSA to South America with only $450 (need $500), the game showed the full untested medicine warning dialog first ("25% chance of adverse effects..."), let me confirm, and THEN said "Insufficient funds! Need $500, have $450". The player goes through the dramatic risk-assessment decision only to be told they can't afford it. The funds check should happen before or alongside the warning, not after.

**You can attempt to treat 0 infected.** In the deploy target selection for South America, "Treat infected (Strain Alpha)" was offered even though Alpha has 0 infected there. Selecting it proceeds to the confirmation step. The game should either hide options with 0 targets, grey them out, or at least warn that there are no targets.

**Defeat triggers at exactly 10.0%.** The defeat screen at tick 1811 showed "Total Dead: 784.8M (10.0% of population)". The per-region percentages ranged from 7.0% (Africa) to 16.0% (Oceania) -- the check is global, not per-region. The game stops simulation on the exact tick the threshold is crossed, which feels precise and fair.

**Victory/defeat number accuracy.** Victory screen (game 777): region deaths sum to approximately 66,508 (0 + 2100 + 0 + 0 + 64400 + 8), total displayed as 66.5K. Defeat screen (game 888): region deaths sum to 784.9M by adding displayed values, total shows 784.8M. The ~0.1M discrepancy is rounding in display, not a real inconsistency -- each value is independently rounded for display.

**BSA requires knowledge >= 0.75 for ALL targeted diseases.** In game 999, even after two identifies on Alpha (knowledge ~0.68) and one identify on Beta (knowledge ~0.34), BSA development wasn't available. The Bench Research list was completely empty, and the Medicines panel said "No medicines available." This makes the Broad-Spectrum Antiviral harder to unlock than a single-target medicine would be, which is an interesting but potentially frustrating trade-off. The player gets no feedback about WHY there are no bench projects -- no message like "Insufficient knowledge to develop any medicines."

**Per-disease clinical trials for broad-spectrum medicines.** BSA requires separate clinical trials per disease it targets. I completed the trial for BSA vs Beta, then deployed to treat Alpha -- and got the untested warning because the Alpha trial wasn't done. This is nuanced and correct, but could confuse players who think "I tested this medicine already."

## What I Wanted To Do But Couldn't

**See WHY bench research is empty.** When all medicines require more knowledge than I have, the Bench Research list just shows nothing. I wanted to see "Develop: Broad-Spectrum Antiviral (requires 75% knowledge of all targets -- Strain Alpha: 68%, Strain Beta: 34%)" or similar.

**Filter deploy targets by relevance.** Showing "Treat infected (Strain Alpha): 0 infected" as a selectable option clutters the deploy screen. I wanted to only see targets where the action would actually do something.

**See a warning about deploying to 0 targets.** Even if the option is shown, selecting "Treat 0 infected" should probably warn or block rather than proceeding to the confirmation flow.

## Ideas

1. **Move the funds check before the untested warning.** When deploying an untested medicine, check funds first. If insufficient, show "Insufficient funds" immediately without making the player go through the risk-assessment warning. The order should be: select target -> check funds -> show untested warning (if applicable) -> deploy.

2. **Show knowledge requirements in Bench Research.** When the bench research list would be empty because no medicines meet the knowledge threshold, show the medicines with a "locked" indicator and the specific knowledge gaps. This helps the player understand what research to prioritize.

3. **Hide or disable zero-target deploy options.** In the deploy target selection, don't show "Treat infected (Strain X)" when there are 0 infected of that strain in the selected region. Or at minimum, show the option greyed out with "(no targets)" instead of letting the player select it and go through the confirmation flow.
