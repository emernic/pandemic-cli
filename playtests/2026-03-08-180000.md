# Playtest -- 2026-03-08 18:00

Seed: 7777 | Ticks played: ~1000

## The Experience

Focused playtest of the World Status population stats feature (issue #37). The old static "Pop: X" line has been replaced with dynamic "Inf X Dead Y" numbers that update as diseases spread. Played from tick 0 through tick 1000, checking display at multiple stages.

The feature works well overall. At tick 0, uninfected regions show "Pop: 500.0M" and infected regions show "Inf 50.0K" (no dead shown when dead is zero). As time advances, dead counts appear and both numbers grow. By tick 500, numbers reach hundreds of millions and still fit cleanly within the box width. The number formatting (K, M, B suffixes) keeps everything compact.

## What Stuck With Me

**The transition from Pop to Inf/Dead is satisfying.** When a region first gets infected, it switches from the calm gray "Pop: 500.0M" to red "Inf 2" and you immediately feel the urgency. Good design choice.

**Header totals match.** I manually added up the regional Inf and Dead numbers at tick 500 and they matched the header bar totals (Infected: 6.3M, Dead: 343.1M). Consistency is solid.

**Selected region detail view can overflow with multiple diseases.** At tick 500, Europe has two diseases. When selected, the box shows "Unknown Pathogen #1" with its stats on the next line, then "Unknown Pathogen #2" on the third line, but Pathogen #2's stats are cut off by the box boundary. At tick 1000, North America has the same issue. The box has only ~3 usable lines below the health bar, and two diseases need 4 lines (name + stats for each). This is a real display limitation.

**Minor "Dead 0" display oddity.** At tick 0, infected regions show "Inf 50.0K" with no dead (dead is zero, hidden). But at tick 100, Africa shows "Inf 2 Dead 0". Looking at the code, this happens because dead is a float -- a value like 0.3 passes the `dead > 0.0` check but `format_number` rounds it to "0". Not a real bug, just a cosmetic edge case from using floating-point population counts.

**Casing inconsistency between summary and detail views.** The summary line uses "Inf" and "Dead" (title case), while the selected region detail uses "inf" and "dead" (lowercase). Example: summary shows "Inf 16.9M Dead 11.8M" but detail shows "16.9M inf 11.8M dead". Might be intentional to differentiate the two contexts, but it caught my eye.

## What I Wanted To Do But Couldn't

Nothing major for this focused test. The feature does what it's supposed to do.

I did want to see immune counts somewhere in the region box. When medicines eventually get deployed and people become immune, there's no indication of that in the summary line. You'd need to look at the health bar color proportions to infer it.

## Ideas

- **Fix the multi-disease detail overflow.** When a selected region has 2+ diseases, the detail lines get clipped. Options: (a) make boxes taller, (b) only show stats for first N diseases, (c) collapse to a compact one-line-per-disease format like "Pathogen #1: 112.6K/75.2K".
- **Hide "Dead 0" display.** Either check if `format_number(dead) == "0"` before showing it, or round dead to integer before the > 0 check.
- **Consider unifying casing.** Either both views use "Inf/Dead" or both use "inf/dead". Small polish item.
