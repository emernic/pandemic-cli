# Playtest -- 2026-03-08 09:33:09

Seed: 7777 (141 ticks, victory), 8888 (506 ticks, victory) | Ticks played: ~650 total

## The Experience

Played two full games to victory. The first (seed 7777) was a fast win at tick 141 -- identified both diseases, developed BSA, treated the three infected regions. The second (seed 8888) was a long grind to tick 506, where I experimented with vaccination, clinical trials, and the endgame of mopping up small outbreaks across multiple regions.

The core loop works well: identify threats, develop medicine, deploy. The tension comes from watching infections and deaths climb while you wait for research to complete. The first game felt tight and satisfying. The second game had a painful late-game stretch where I was repeatedly waiting 50-100 ticks for $500 to accumulate so I could treat 4-140 people in a region -- same $500 flat cost whether treating 266K or 4.

## What Stuck With Me

**The "No infected population in Asia" message is misleading.** When deploying BSA to treat Strain Beta in Asia (which has 266K infected with Strain Alpha but 0 with Strain Beta), the status bar says "No infected population in Asia." This is factually wrong -- there ARE infected people in Asia, just not with Strain Beta. The message should specify the disease name, e.g., "No Strain Beta infected in Asia."

**BSA shows [UNTESTED] even after completing one clinical trial.** I ran "Trial: BSA vs Strain Alpha" which completed successfully. But the medicines panel still shows "[UNTESTED]" with no indication that it's partially tested. When I then deployed BSA to treat Strain Alpha, it went through without an untested warning -- so the per-disease trial tracking works correctly in the engine. But the UI gives no feedback about partial test status. A player who runs one trial and still sees [UNTESTED] would reasonably think the trial didn't work.

**Antiviral-A has no strategic reason to exist.** Both Antiviral-A and BSA cost 30 RP, 10 personnel, 40 ticks to develop. BSA targets both diseases; Antiviral-A targets only Strain Alpha. Same development cost, strictly worse output. There is no scenario where developing Antiviral-A is the better choice.

**Vaccination creates a confusing region card entry.** After vaccinating North America against Strain Alpha (which had 0 infections), the region card showed "Strain Alpha / Inf 0". This looks like the disease arrived and was contained, not like a proactive vaccination. There is no indication of how many people were immunized or that vaccination occurred at all.

## What I Wanted To Do But Couldn't

**See immunity counts on the region cards.** After vaccinating NA, I wanted to see "500K immune" somewhere on the card. The threats panel shows immune counts, but the region cards on the map only show infected and dead.

**Treat multiple small outbreaks cheaply.** Paying $500 to treat 4 people in Europe felt absurd. The flat pricing creates a tedious endgame where you wait for funds to trickle in to mop up tiny infections one region at a time. I wanted a way to deploy smaller, cheaper doses.

**See partial clinical trial status on the medicine.** After trialing BSA against Strain Alpha, I wanted the medicine to show something like "[TESTED: Strain Alpha] [UNTESTED: Strain Beta]" instead of just "[UNTESTED]".

**Navigate the research panel without losing my place.** Going from field research back to categories and then to bench research requires Esc-Esc-down-Enter, and getting the Esc count wrong closes the panel entirely. A shortcut to switch between field and bench would help.

## Ideas

1. **Fix the zero-target deployment message** to include the disease name: "No Strain Beta infected in Asia" instead of "No infected population in Asia."

2. **Show partial clinical trial status** on the medicines panel. Either "Tested for: Strain Alpha" or split the tag into per-disease badges.

3. **Consider variable deployment costs** -- or at least a minimum viable deployment for small outbreaks. The flat $500 to treat 4 people makes the endgame a tedious fund-waiting exercise.

4. **Differentiate Antiviral-A from BSA** in cost or capability. If BSA is strictly superior at the same price, Antiviral-A is a trap option that wastes player resources.

5. **Show vaccination/immunity on region cards** so players can see the effect of preventive deployment.
