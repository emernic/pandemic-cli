# Playtest -- 2026-03-08 16:01

Seed: 592530324 | Ticks played: ~168 | Persona: The Explorer

## The Experience

I opened every panel before taking a single action. The game starts you with two unknown pathogens, zero research points, and a ticking clock. With this seed, Oceania had 35.1K infected from the start and Asia had 362 -- so the urgency was clearly on Oceania.

The first 10 ticks are forced waiting. You have 0 RP and need 10 to start identifying. This felt fine at real-time pace (5 seconds), but as an explorer who wanted to *do something* right away, I noticed the gap. I tried to start research with 0 RP -- the game correctly showed "Insufficient resources!" on the confirm screen. Good guardrail.

I decided to rush narrow medicines: identify Strain Alpha twice (to 100% knowledge), develop Antiviral-A (15 RP, 25 ticks -- the new cheaper narrow cost), skip clinical trial, and deploy immediately. The strategy worked but came with real costs: the untested medicine killed 20,000 people in Oceania via adverse reaction. That was a genuine dramatic moment.

Meanwhile I aggressively enabled all three policies in Oceania (Travel Ban, Quarantine, Hospital Surge = $23/tick) and Travel Ban + Quarantine in Asia ($18/tick). Total policy drain: $41/tick against $5/tick income. This caused a funding crisis at tick 40 -- "FUNDING CRISIS: All policies suspended!" -- which was clearly communicated. The disease immediately started spreading faster without containment.

By tick 75, I had Antiviral-A developed and tried to deploy to Oceania. Cost $200, I only had $186. The game correctly blocked it: "Insufficient funds! Need $200, have $186". I waited 3 ticks, deployed vaccination to Oceania (100K doses, adverse reaction killed 20K), then vaccinated North America. The strain drift system was visible here: "Strain outdated! (50% strain match -- re-trial to update)" since the virus had mutated to Gen 4.

By tick 168, Oceania had 1.6M infected and 522.6K dead. The vaccination was too little too late for that region, but North America was contained at 63 infected with 50K immune. The strategy of proactive vaccination to uninfected regions felt meaningful and smart.

## What Stuck With Me

**The knowledge-gated information is genuinely interesting.** At tick 30, the map showed Asia with "Inf 1.3K" but the threats panel only attributed 9 of those to Strain Alpha. The rest was Strain Beta, which I couldn't see details on yet. This created genuine uncertainty -- exactly the right feeling for an explorer.

**The adverse reaction system creates real drama.** Deploying untested medicine and seeing "ADVERSE REACTION: 20.0K died" is a punch in the gut. The warning screen before deployment is clear and well-written ("25% chance of adverse effects -- 20% of doses will KILL instead of help"). This is one of the best risk/reward moments in the game.

**The funding crisis mechanic works well as a guardrail** but feels like a binary cliff. One tick you have all policies running, the next tick they're all gone. There's no partial degradation -- no "your quarantine is underfunded so it's only 60% effective."

**The strain drift / mutation system creates real ongoing tension.** Having my medicine go from 100% to 50% effective doses because the virus mutated is a great "oh no" moment. The "Strain outdated!" warning on the deploy screen is clear. The clinical trial re-appearing in the field research menu after drift is discoverable.

**Narrow vs broad-spectrum costs feel meaningfully different now.** Antiviral-A at 15 RP / 25 ticks vs Broad-Spectrum at 40 RP / 50 ticks -- the narrow medicine was ready almost twice as fast for a third of the RP cost. This made the rush strategy viable and created a real choice.

## What I Wanted To Do But Couldn't

**See Strain Beta's region-level data at 50% knowledge.** The threats panel shows Strain Beta's infectivity (3%) and type (Bacterium) but no region breakdown. I could infer from the map that it was in Asia (~1.3K - 9 = ~1.3K from Beta), but I wished the threats panel would show "Present in: Asia" even at 50% knowledge, even without exact numbers.

**Compare medicine options side-by-side before committing.** When choosing between Antiviral-A (narrow, cheap, fast) and Broad-Spectrum (covers both diseases, expensive, slow), I had to remember the costs from one screen to the next. A summary showing both options on the same screen would help.

**See the total running cost of policies across all regions from the top-level policy view.** The policy panel does show "Policy cost: $41/tick" at the top, which is great. But I wanted to see my net income/burn rate somewhere permanent -- something like "Income: $5/tick, Policies: -$41/tick, Net: -$36/tick" in the resource bar.

**Deploy medicine to multiple regions in quick succession without navigating back through the full menu.** After deploying to Oceania, the UI drops me back to region selection for the same medicine -- that's good design. But I couldn't afford a second deployment anyway. When I could afford it later, I had to re-open the medicines panel from scratch.

**See the map update in real-time as I sit on a deploy screen.** The numbers on the deploy screen are stale from when I opened it. The region data updates if I advance ticks with the panel open, but it's easy to forget the situation is changing while you deliberate.

## Ideas

**Graduated policy failure instead of binary crisis.** Instead of all policies instantly suspending when funds hit zero, policies could degrade:
- At 75% funding: policies operate at reduced effectiveness (quarantine only halves infection rate instead of full effect).
- At 50% funding: you get a warning, non-essential policies start auto-suspending (cheapest first).
- At 0% funding: everything suspends.
This creates a period of partial control where you're scrambling to cut costs, rather than a sudden cliff. The player would have to actively choose which policies to sacrifice, which is more interesting than having them all yanked away.

**Medicine deployment cost scaling with doses needed.** Right now deploying costs a flat $200 whether you vaccinate 14 people or 500K people. What if the cost scaled with the number of effective doses used? Something like $50 base + $1 per 1K doses. This would make early vaccination of low-infection regions cheap (good strategy), while mass vaccination of a hot zone is expensive (realistic). It would also give the player more granular funding decisions instead of "can I afford the flat $200?"

**Visible trade route connections on the map that show active spread.** The map already shows connections between regions (the --- lines). What if when a disease is actively spreading along a connection, the line showed a directional indicator? Something like "--->" or "==>". Then the travel ban policy could visibly sever that connection (showing "---X---"). This would make the map the primary strategic view where you can see the flow of disease and decide where to intervene.

**A "forecast" or projection when hovering over a region.** At the current growth rate, Oceania will hit X infected in Y ticks. Something like "Doubling time: 15 ticks" or "At current rate: 5M infected in 50 ticks." This would help players understand the urgency without having to manually track numbers between ticks.

**Income sources tied to region health.** Currently income is a flat $5/tick regardless of how the world is doing. What if each healthy region contributed to income? Healthy region: $2/tick, infected region: $1/tick, critical region: $0/tick. This creates a death spiral (losing regions means losing income means losing ability to fight) that parallels real pandemic economics. It would also make "saving" a region feel economically rewarding, not just morally satisfying.
