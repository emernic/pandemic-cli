# Playtest -- 2026-03-08 04:03

Seed: 7777 | Ticks played: ~535

## The Experience

I started the game and immediately found myself 50 ticks in with two unknown diseases already spreading. Asia was critical with 219K infected. I spent my first 60 ticks identifying Strain Alpha through three rounds of research (each adding 34% knowledge), watching helplessly as infections climbed from 200K to over a million. By the time I had the Antiviral-A clinically tested and ready for safe deployment (~tick 135), there were 2.8M infected and 1.8M dead.

The core problem: my only tools (10K-50K dose medicines) were completely outmatched by exponential disease growth. Each $100 deployment of Antiviral-A treated 10K people against millions infected. I tried the untested route early on and got punished -- deploying untested Antiviral-A to North America killed 2,000 people via adverse effects (20% of doses). That was a genuinely impactful teaching moment.

By tick 335, every region was CRIT. By tick 535, 348M dead. Asia alone lost 291M. The disease eventually started burning out through herd immunity (1.5B immune in Asia) but the damage was catastrophic. Resources piled up uselessly -- I ended with $2,975 and 490 RP with nothing meaningful to spend them on.

The experience felt like watching a slow-motion catastrophe with a squirt gun.

## What Stuck With Me

**The untested medicine consequence was great.** Deploying untested Antiviral-A to North America and seeing 2K dead from adverse effects (when there were zero infections there) was a genuine "oh no" moment. The game clearly communicates risk vs. reward.

**The knowledge-gating system works well.** Starting with "Unknown Pathogen #1" at 0% knowledge, gradually revealing the name at 33%, then stats at 66% -- this creates a satisfying progression of understanding.

**The research pipeline is logical.** Field research (identify, clinical trials) vs. bench research (develop medicines) is intuitive. The UI flow through categories to projects to confirmation is clean.

**The region deployment screen for multi-disease medicines is unreadable.** When deploying Broad-Spectrum Antiviral, the region list shows something like "North America 500.0Ms 0i 8.0K checkmark 500.0Ms 0i" -- two diseases' worth of data crammed into one line with no labels indicating which disease is which. This was the most confusing UI element.

**The health bars (solid blocks) don't convey information.** Every region shows a full bar of block characters regardless of how bad the outbreak is. These appear to be purely decorative.

## What I Wanted To Do But Couldn't

**Scale up medicine production.** I wanted to spend my accumulating funds on manufacturing more doses. Having 10K doses against 40M infected felt absurd. I wanted a "fund mass production" option or something that let me scale intervention with my resources.

**Quarantine or travel restrictions.** Watching diseases jump from Asia to Europe to Africa to North America with no ability to slow cross-region spread was frustrating. A policy to close borders (even partially) would give a meaningful strategic lever.

**See a game over or win condition.** The simulation just keeps running. There is no "humanity survived" or "game over" state. After 500 ticks with 348M dead, the game was still going. Without stakes or scoring, the late game felt like watching numbers change with no purpose.

**See deployment feedback.** After deploying medicine, there is no confirmation message or summary of what happened. The infected count changes in the header, but it is easy to miss. An event log or notification like "Deployed 10K doses, treated 10K infected in Asia" would help.

**Access the second disease's research.** I never identified Unknown Pathogen #2 but somehow knew its name was "Strain Beta" because the Medicines panel showed "Broad-Spectrum Antiviral: Strain Alpha, Strain Beta". The medicine names leak information that should be knowledge-gated.

## Ideas

1. **Scale medicine production with funds.** Let players spend money to increase dose counts or manufacture additional batches. This gives funding a real purpose beyond the flat deployment cost.

2. **Add policy mechanics.** Travel restrictions, lockdowns, research funding boosts -- anything that gives players strategic levers beyond "deploy small medicine doses." This is the biggest gap in gameplay.

3. **Win/lose conditions.** Define what success looks like (e.g., total deaths under a threshold, diseases eradicated) and what failure looks like (e.g., total population drops below X). Give the player stakes.

4. **Fix the multi-disease region display.** When deploying a medicine that targets multiple diseases, label which set of numbers belongs to which disease. Current format is incomprehensible.

5. **Make health bars functional.** Use the bar to show something useful -- percentage of population infected, or ratio of infected to immune, or infection trend.

6. **Add an event log or notifications.** "Strain Alpha has spread to Europe," "Clinical trial completed," "Deployed 10K doses to Asia." The game has a lot of empty space at the bottom of the world map that could show recent events.

7. **Knowledge-gate the infection numbers.** At 0% knowledge, the threats panel still shows exact infected/immune/dead counts. This data should be hidden or approximate ("??? infected" or "~200K infected") until some knowledge threshold.

8. **Fix medicine name leaking disease identity.** "Broad-Spectrum Antiviral: Strain Alpha, Strain Beta" reveals the name of Strain Beta even if it has not been identified. Show "Unknown Pathogen #2" in the medicine description if that disease has not been identified.
