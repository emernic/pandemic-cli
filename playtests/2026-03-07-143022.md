# Playtest -- 2026-03-07 14:30

Seed: 1234 | Ticks played: ~120

## First Impressions

The game starts paused, which is good -- it gives you a moment to orient. The top status bar is immediately readable: you can see tick count, funds, RP, personnel, and aggregate infected/dead counts at a glance. The region table is clean and easy to scan. The hotkey bar at the bottom clearly tells you what keys are available.

However, within about 30 seconds of exploring, you realize there is nothing to *do*. Three of the four panels (Research, Medicines, Policy) say "Coming soon." The only functional panel is Threats, which shows disease stats but offers no actions. So the entire play session is: unpause, watch numbers go up, check Threats occasionally.

## What Worked

- **Status bar layout**: The top bar packs a lot of info into one line without feeling cramped. PAUSED/RUNNING state is clearly visible.
- **Region table**: Clean columns, readable number formatting (K/M/B suffixes), risk levels (OK/LOW/MOD/HIGH/CRIT) give a quick sense of severity.
- **Threats panel**: Shows useful info -- infectivity, lethality, which regions are affected. The region list updates as the disease spreads.
- **Hotkey bar**: Always visible, context-sensitive (shows "Resume" when paused, "Pause" when running). Good.
- **Panel toggling**: Opening/closing panels with single keys works smoothly. ESC to close is intuitive.
- **Number formatting**: The K/M/B suffixes are well-chosen. "270.7M" is much more readable than "270,700,000".

## What Didn't Work

- **No player agency**: This is the biggest issue. You can watch the disease spread but cannot do anything about it. Funds accumulate ($1600 by tick 120), research points pile up (120 RP), personnel sit idle (50) -- all with no way to spend them. The game is currently a screensaver, not a game.
- **"-0" display bug**: Uninfected regions show "-0" for both Infected and Dead columns instead of "0" or "--" or blank. This looks like a floating-point formatting artifact and is visually confusing.
- **Cross-region spread is very slow**: It took ~55-60 ticks for the disease to spread from Asia to even one other region. By tick 120, North America and South America were still completely uninfected while Asia had 1.4 billion infected. The spread rate between regions feels unrealistically slow compared to in-region growth (logistic growth within Asia is very fast).
- **No events or narrative**: Nothing happens besides numbers ticking up. No news events, no milestones, no alerts when a new region gets infected. The game tells you nothing proactively -- you have to notice changes yourself.
- **Massive empty space**: The region table has 6 rows but the panel takes up the entire screen height (~40 rows). Most of the UI is empty box. This space could show charts, event logs, or other useful information.
- **Personnel never changes**: Stays at 50 the entire game. Unclear what it's for.

## Suggestions

1. **Implement at least one action** (highest impact): Even a simple "deploy quarantine to region" that costs funds and reduces spread rate would transform this from a passive viewer into a game. The resource accumulation system is already there -- it just needs something to spend resources on.

2. **Add an event log / news ticker**: A scrolling list at the bottom or in the empty space showing things like "Tick 57: Strain Alpha detected in Europe" or "Tick 80: Asia infections exceed 10M" would make the simulation feel alive and give the player information without requiring them to stare at the table.

3. **Fix the "-0" display**: Show "0", "--", or leave blank for regions with zero infections. The "-0" looks like a bug.

4. **Speed up cross-region spread**: The current spread rate makes most of the world feel irrelevant for the first 100 ticks. Consider making spread probability proportional to the source region's infection count, or adding travel-route-based spread.

5. **Use the empty vertical space**: The region table could show per-region infection trend (up arrow, down arrow), or the bottom half could show a simple text-based graph of total infections over time.

6. **Add alerts/notifications**: When a new region gets infected, or when a risk level changes, highlight the row or flash the status bar. Important state changes should be hard to miss.
