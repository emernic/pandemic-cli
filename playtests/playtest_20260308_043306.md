# Playtest -- 2026-03-08 04:33

Seed: 8899 | Ticks played: ~1260

## The Experience

I started the game paused at tick 0 with two unknown pathogens. One was in Asia (50K infected), the other in South America (500 infected). I spent the first 70 ticks researching -- identifying both pathogens through the research pipeline. By tick 110 I had developed Antiviral-A and deployed 10K doses to treat infected in Asia. It was a drop in the bucket against 1.3M infected.

From there, I mostly watched the world burn. Diseases spread slowly at first -- Oceania got its first infection around tick 110, Europe and Africa around tick 140-160. By tick 460, 338M were dead. By tick 960, over a billion. By tick 1260, 1.4 billion dead. The diseases eventually burned out in each region through herd immunity, but at catastrophic cost.

The game never ended. No win condition, no loss condition. Resources piled up unused ($7050, 1165 RP) because there was nothing meaningful to spend them on fast enough. The medicines were a tiny bandage on a massive wound.

## What Stuck With Me

**The research project replacement problem (likely a bug).** When I started a field research project (Identify Pathogen #2), then immediately started another field project (Identify Strain Alpha), the first one was silently cancelled. No warning, no refund of the 10 RP I spent. The UI just replaced the active project. This felt like a significant UX problem -- either prevent starting a second project in the same slot (with a clear message), or at minimum warn the player.

**Health bars never change.** From tick 0 to tick 1260, every health bar was a solid green block, regardless of how many people were infected or dead. Asia had 868M dead (18% of its 4.7B population) and the bar was still solid green. This appears to be a rendering bug -- the bars seem to be decorative rather than functional.

**The knowledge-gated region display is confusing at first.** Unknown Pathogen #2 showed no region data in the threats panel even though I could see "Inf 500" in South America on the map. It took a moment to realize this was because knowledge was at 0%. Not a bug, but the disconnect between what the map shows and what the threats panel shows is a little jarring.

**Strain Beta appeared to be in South America on the region card, but the threats breakdown at 34% knowledge didn't show any region data.** Meanwhile North America showed 248K infected on the map but wasn't listed under either disease in the threats panel. This made it hard to understand which disease was where.

## What I Wanted To Do But Couldn't

**See the actual effect of deploying medicine.** When I deployed 10K doses of Antiviral-A to treat infected in Asia, there was zero feedback. No "10K people treated" message, no visible change. I had to just trust that something happened.

**Understand what the health bars represent.** They never changed, so I have no idea what they're supposed to convey. Population health? Disease severity? They're just green blocks.

**Do something meaningful during the 20-40 tick research wait times.** While waiting for projects to complete, the only action is to watch numbers grow. There's no policy lever to pull, no resource allocation to adjust, no triage decision to make. The game becomes a spectator experience between research completions.

**Know when research completed.** There's no notification or event log. The project just silently disappears from the active list. I had to check the research panel periodically to see if things finished.

**Have any kind of end state.** 1.4 billion people died and the game kept running. There's no score, no win, no loss. The session just continues indefinitely.

## Ideas

1. **Fix the health bars.** This seems like a rendering bug. The bars should visually represent the ratio of healthy/infected/dead population. Even a simple red-green gradient would add a lot of information at a glance.

2. **Block or warn on research slot replacement.** If a field project is running and the player tries to start another field project, either refuse it outright (with a clear "Field slot occupied" message) or show a confirmation dialog explaining the active project will be cancelled and RP will NOT be refunded.

3. **Add an event log or notification system.** "Research completed: Identify Strain Alpha" / "Disease arrived in Europe" / "10K doses deployed in Asia (8.2K treated, 1.8K adverse effects)". This would transform the experience from guessing to informed decision-making.

4. **Add win/loss conditions.** Even simple ones: "Lose if dead > X" or "Win if infected reaches 0 globally." Without these, the game has no stakes.

5. **Consider faster medicine dose scaling.** 10K doses against millions of infected is meaningless. Either make doses cheaper/larger, or let the player produce more doses through research/funding.
