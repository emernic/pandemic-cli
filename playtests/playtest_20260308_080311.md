# Playtest -- 2026-03-08 08:03

Seed: 42 (victory), 99 (defeat) | Ticks played: ~111 (win), ~1884 (loss)

## The Experience

### Session 1: Winning (Seed 42)

Played strategically to win as fast as possible. Started with 50K infected in Asia, 500 in South America. The core gameplay loop was: accumulate RP, identify diseases, develop medicine, deploy. Won at tick 111 with 128.4K dead.

The game felt satisfying to play once I understood the research pipeline. Identifying pathogens twice gave enough knowledge (0.68) to unlock medicine development in bench research. I chose the Broad-Spectrum Antiviral because it targets both diseases, which seemed smart. The clinical trial step was tempting to skip, and I did -- deployed untested medicine three times to clear all infections. Got an adverse reaction in Asia (37.2K killed from the medicine itself) which was genuinely tense.

### Session 2: Losing (Seed 99)

Let the game run with zero player intervention. Defeat triggered at tick 1884 when deaths hit 10% of population (784.2M dead). The diseases spread across all 6 regions and killed relentlessly.

## What Stuck With Me

**Victory summary screen (#98) -- works well.** Clean layout with duration, total dead, survivors, immunized, still infected, and per-region breakdown. The message "All diseases eradicated. Humanity is saved." feels appropriately triumphant.

**Defeat summary screen (#98) -- also works well.** "Humanity has fallen. Too many lives were lost." is somber. The stats clearly show the scale of destruction. Defeat triggers at exactly 10.0% population dead.

**Research UI target diseases (#100) -- verified and clear.** When browsing bench research, each medicine project shows "Targets: Strain Alpha" or "Targets: Strain Alpha, Strain Beta" right under the project name. This makes the choice between narrow and broad-spectrum medicine obvious.

**Game-over action blocking (#103) -- working correctly.** After both victory and defeat:
- Research panel opens for browsing but Enter does not drill into categories or start projects
- Medicine panel opens for browsing but Enter does not initiate deployment
- This was tested on both the victory (seed 42) and defeat (seed 99) states

**Negative zero fix (#99) -- no negative zeros visible.** All percentages display correctly: "0.0%" not "-0.0%". Checked particularly in the victory summary where most regions had near-zero deaths.

**Adverse reaction mechanic is dramatic.** Deploying an untested medicine and seeing "ADVERSE REACTION: 37.2K died" in the status bar hits hard. The warning dialog with the 25%/20% stats gives enough info to make an informed gamble.

## What I Wanted To Do But Couldn't

**No feedback that Enter is blocked after game over.** The UI still shows "[Enter] Select" hints in research and medicine panels after game over, but pressing Enter does nothing. A disabled/grayed-out hint or a brief flash message like "Game is over" would be clearer.

**Cannot review game history.** After winning/losing, I wanted to see what happened during the game -- when diseases spread to new regions, when I deployed medicine, etc. A log or timeline would be nice.

## Ideas

1. **Gray out or remove "[Enter] Select" hints after game over.** The current UI hints suggest you can still act, even though the actions are correctly blocked. Change the hint text or dim it.

2. **Add a simple event log.** Even just the last 10 events ("Disease spread to Europe", "Deployed medicine to Asia", "Research complete") would add narrative flavor and help players understand what happened.

3. **Show adverse reaction risk on the medicine list itself.** The "[UNTESTED]" tag is there, but the 25% chance info only appears at the confirmation step. Showing "25% risk" earlier would help decision-making.
