# Playtest -- 2026-03-08 14:01

Seed: 7749 | Ticks played: ~540

## The Experience

I played a full game loop from tick 0 to tick 540. The early game had a nice rhythm: two unknown pathogens appeared, one huge (50K in Asia) and one small (500 in South America). I immediately felt urgency to research them. The research pipeline -- identify, develop medicine, deploy -- felt intuitive and satisfying to work through. I identified Strain Alpha, got it to 100% knowledge, developed the Broad-Spectrum Antiviral, and deployed it to Asia at tick 90, wiping out 146.5K infections in one shot. That moment felt powerful and rewarding.

But after that big moment, the game lost its tension. The second disease (Unknown Pathogen #2) spread at a glacial pace -- single-digit infections in 4 regions for hundreds of ticks. I never felt urgency to deal with it because the numbers were so small. I spent most of my time just advancing ticks and watching numbers barely move. By tick 540, it had only reached 1.6K total infected across 3 regions. Meanwhile, I was sitting on $1700 and 490 RP with nothing to spend them on.

The first seed I tried (without --ticks 0) auto-advanced to tick 121 and showed VICTORY -- the diseases had burned themselves out with 106K dead and zero player intervention needed. That is a major balance issue: the game can be "won" by doing literally nothing.

## What Stuck With Me

**The untested medicine warning was great.** When I deployed the Broad-Spectrum Antiviral and got the warning about 25% chance of adverse effects killing 20% of patients, I genuinely hesitated. That is a real decision moment. In this case nothing bad happened, but the tension was there.

**The research pipeline feels good conceptually.** Unknown -> Identify -> Develop -> Deploy is a clear, satisfying progression. Seeing "Unknown Pathogen #1" become "Strain Alpha" with visible stats felt like real progress.

**Disease spread is too slow to create drama.** Strain Alpha in Asia grew from 50K to 146K over 90 ticks, which felt about right. But the cross-region spread was painfully slow -- single-digit infections that took hundreds of ticks to reach even 1K. The second disease (500 starting in South America) never felt threatening. In a real-time game at 500ms/tick, 100 ticks is under a minute, so this pacing might feel different -- but even then, the small outbreaks barely register.

**Navigation through the medicine deployment wizard is tedious.** Selecting a region requires counting down through a list (North America, South America, Europe, Africa, Asia, Oceania). Selecting a treatment target requires counting down through another list (Vaccinate Strain Alpha, Vaccinate Pathogen #2, Treat Strain Alpha, Treat Pathogen #2). Deploying to 3 regions means navigating through roughly 20 key presses. The "return to region select after deploy" is a good UX choice, but the cursor resets to the top each time.

**The game runs while you navigate panels.** This is subtle but I noticed infections changing between key presses. In real-time play this would feel natural, but it means the numbers on screen can be stale by the time you make a decision. Not necessarily a bug, but worth noting.

**Region cards showing disease names is useful.** North America showing "Unknown Pathogen #2 / Inf 1" on the map card is a nice touch -- I can see at a glance what is happening where without opening the Threats panel.

## What I Wanted To Do But Couldn't

**I wanted to treat all regions at once.** After deploying to Asia, I had to individually navigate to each of 5 other regions and repeat the same deploy flow. A "deploy to all affected regions" option would save a lot of tedium.

**I wanted to run a clinical trial before deploying.** The game warns you about untested medicine, but there was no obvious way to test it short of just deploying it. I could have gone to Field Research and started a clinical trial (15 RP, 5 personnel, 25 ticks), but I never saw it offered in the Field Research list -- maybe because I needed to have the medicine developed first and it would appear later? The pipeline step was unclear.

**I wanted to see Strain Alpha's full stats before deploying.** The Threats panel showed "Infect: 6%, Lethal: ?, Recov: ?" at 50% knowledge. I could not see lethality or recovery until I did the second identify round to reach 100%. But I had to make deployment decisions at 50% knowledge -- it would be nice to have partial info earlier.

**I wanted the Policy panel to do something.** Travel restrictions, quarantines, public health campaigns -- these would give me something to do during the long wait while research completes.

**I wanted to see which disease was actually infecting each region.** The Threats panel shows per-region breakdowns, but the map cards only sometimes show disease names. Europe showed "Inf 605" but did not specify which disease(s) were responsible. The Threats panel showed it was all Strain Alpha, but I had to check the panel to confirm.

## Ideas

**Make cross-region spread faster and more dangerous.** The current spread rate makes secondary outbreaks trivial. What if spread probability scaled with the number of infected in the source region? Asia at 100K infected should be raining disease on every connected region, not trickling 1 person per 10 ticks. The formula could be: spread_chance = base_rate * log(infected_in_source) / 10, so large outbreaks create explosive secondary waves. This creates the Plague Inc. feeling of watching the map light up red, which is exactly the tension the player needs to feel.

**Add a "mass deploy" or "global campaign" option.** When you have 500K doses and infections in 5 regions totaling 2K people, let the player spend extra money (say, 2x the normal cost) to deploy globally in one action. This removes the tedium of per-region deployment without removing the per-region option for when you want surgical precision.

**The clinical trial step needs clearer integration.** After developing a medicine, the Field Research panel should prominently offer the clinical trial option, perhaps with a notification: "New medicine developed -- clinical trial available." Right now it is easy to miss that clinical trials exist. The untested warning on deploy is good, but the path to testing should be more discoverable.

**Victory needs to require player action.** The fact that the game can reach VICTORY with zero player input is a fundamental problem. Victory should require eradicating diseases through medicine deployment, not just waiting for them to burn out naturally. One approach: diseases should not burn out on their own -- recovered individuals should have a chance of relapse, or new waves should emerge. Alternatively, victory could require vaccinating a threshold percentage of the susceptible population, not just reducing infections to zero.

**Resource accumulation needs sinks.** By tick 540 I had 490 RP and $1700 with nothing to spend them on. The game needs ongoing costs: personnel salaries, medicine manufacturing, hospital maintenance, public communication campaigns. What if maintaining a regional hospital cost $50/tick and 3 personnel, reduced lethality by 20% in that region, but you had to actively choose where to build them? Now you have a spatial resource allocation puzzle that persists through the whole game, not just the research phase.

**The mid-game needs events and escalation.** After the initial research-and-deploy rush, the game goes quiet. Periodic events would sustain engagement: disease mutations that increase infectivity or resistance, supply chain disruptions that reduce medicine doses, political events that cut funding, new outbreaks in cleared regions from travelers. Each event should force the player to re-evaluate their strategy, not just react to the same slow spread.

**Show a timeline or graph of infection progression.** A small sparkline or trend indicator per region would help the player understand whether infections are growing, stable, or declining without having to remember previous numbers. Even just an up/down arrow next to the infection count would add useful information.
