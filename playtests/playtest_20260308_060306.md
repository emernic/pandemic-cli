Seed: 12345 (with comparison checks on seed 99999) | Ticks played: ~2000

## The Experience

Started with two diseases: Strain Alpha (50K in Asia) and Strain Beta (500 in S. America). Spent the first 120 ticks identifying threats and watching diseases spread region by region. By tick 160 I had Antiviral-A developed but untested. Deployed it once to treat Asia (10K doses vs 5.5M infected -- a drop in the bucket) and got an adverse reaction that killed 2K people. After that, there was nothing meaningful left to do. Resources piled up endlessly ($10,750 and 1,930 RP by tick 2000) while 1.4 billion people died. The diseases eventually burned out through herd immunity around tick 1500-2000.

## What Stuck With Me

### Save/Load Round-Trip: Perfect
Saved at tick 50 mid-research (Strain Alpha identification at 50%), loaded the copy, and both copies produced identical results at tick 60: Infected 299.5K, Dead 162.5K, Funds $1300, RP 40. The seeded RNG state serializes correctly. Determinism is fully preserved across save/load boundaries.

### Seeds Don't Differentiate Enough
Seeds 12345 and 99999 produce the same initial state (identical layout, populations, starting infections). At tick 10, Asia had 66.9K vs 67.3K infected, S. America 776 vs 773. The seed only introduces slight RNG variation in spread/death rolls. It does not affect the starting scenario at all. Different seeds feel like the same game.

### Threat Level Thresholds (from code and observation)
- OK: 0 infected
- LOW: >0 infected (even 1 person)
- MOD: >1,000 infected
- HIGH: >10,000 infected
- CRIT: >100,000 infected

These are consistent and sensible. However, see the floating-point bug below.

### Floating-Point Threat Level Bug (NEW)
At tick 2000, all diseases have burned out. The header shows "Infected: 0" and every region card displays "Inf 0". But all six regions are stuck at threat level LOW instead of OK. The threat check is `infected > 0.0` which evaluates true for tiny floating-point remnants (e.g., 0.0000001) that round to "0" in the display. This means regions can never return to OK status once infected, even after the disease is completely gone. The game's "steady state" after a pandemic is all-LOW everywhere, which feels wrong.

### Deploying to Regions With 0 Infected (Edge Case)
I deployed Antiviral-A "Treat infected (Strain Alpha)" to South America, which had 0 Strain Alpha infections. The deployment went through without charging any funds ($1800 before and after). No error message, no feedback that it was pointless. The next deployment to Asia (5.5M infected) correctly charged $250. So deploying treatment to 0 infected is a no-op that doesn't cost anything -- arguably it should either be blocked or at least show a message.

## What I Wanted To Do But Couldn't

1. **See which disease is in which region from the map.** Region cards show total infected/dead but only show disease names for some regions (S. America shows "Strain Beta" and "Strain Alpha" but Asia just shows totals). You have to open Threats to see the per-disease breakdown.

2. **Deploy medicine to multiple regions quickly.** After treating Asia, I was dropped back to the region selection screen for the same medicine, which is good. But with 10K doses vs millions infected, I'd want to deploy dozens of times, and each deployment requires navigating the menu tree.

3. **Know if a deployment actually did anything.** The bottom bar message is good for the adverse reaction case, but there's no persistent indicator of how many people were cured or vaccinated in a region.

4. **Stop the game from feeling hopeless.** By tick 160 when I finally had medicine, Asia had 5.5M infected and my medicine had 10K doses. The math is overwhelmingly against you. There's no sense that player actions can change the outcome.

## Ideas

1. **Fix the floating-point threat level bug.** Round infected to nearest integer before comparing: `if infected.round() > 0.0` or use a small epsilon. Regions should return to OK when the disease burns out.

2. **Make seeds affect starting conditions.** Randomize which regions start with diseases, which diseases exist, starting populations, or at least the initial infection counts. Currently every game starts identically.

3. **Add a "free deployment" guard.** If treating a region with 0 infected, either skip the deployment silently (current behavior minus the visual noise of going through menus) or show "No infected to treat" and block it.

4. **Threat level could incorporate dead count or percentage.** A region showing "Inf 0 Dead 870.9M" as LOW feels misleading. A region that lost 870M people is not "low threat" -- it's devastated. Consider a separate status for post-pandemic regions.
