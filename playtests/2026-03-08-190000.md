# Playtest -- 2026-03-08 19:00 (UI/UX Critique)

Seed: 888 | Ticks played: ~630

## The Experience

I approached this as someone evaluating the interface design, looking at every panel and flow for clarity, consistency, and visual effectiveness. The game has a solid structural foundation -- the two-panel layout (map + detail panel), the hotkey bar, the research/medicine pipeline flow -- but the presentation has significant room for improvement. The most striking issue is the vast amount of wasted screen space: nearly 60% of the screen is empty in most views.

## What Stuck With Me

### Good

- **Panel title updates contextually.** "Field Research" -> "Confirm Research" -> "Antiviral-A -> Asia" -- you always know where you are in the flow.
- **Error states are clear.** "Insufficient resources!" with only [Esc] Back is exactly right. No ambiguity.
- **The research->medicine->deploy pipeline is intuitive.** I never felt lost in the multi-step process. The state machine is well-designed.
- **Medicine deployment returning to region selection** for rapid multi-region deployment is a smart UX choice.
- **Knowledge-gating in threats** reveals information progressively (name at 33%, stats at 66%). Thematically coherent.
- **Hotkey bar** shows the right controls and updates between Pause/Resume. Clean and functional.

### Bad

- **Health bars never change.** Every region shows a full green bar (████████████████████████████) regardless of whether 0 people or 292 million people are dead. This is the single biggest visual failure. The bars are prime real estate for communicating severity at a glance, and they communicate nothing.
- **Region cards are mostly empty.** Each card has room for 4 lines of content below population, but only North America (with 2 diseases) shows any infection text. Regions at CRIT with millions dead show nothing inside the card. The status label (OK/LOW/MOD/HIGH/CRIT) in the top-right corner does almost all the work.
- **The bottom 60% of the screen is wasted.** The map occupies the top ~17 rows of a ~42-row display area. Below the region cards is nothing but empty bordered space. This is true for both the map panel and the detail panel.
- **Region selection in medicine deployment is hard to read.** Lines like `Asia 4.7Bs 2.3Mi 7.6M checkmark` jam numbers together without clear separation. Compare to the Threats panel which uses properly spaced columns. The deployment screen should use the same column formatting.
- **No deployment feedback.** After deploying medicine, funds drop but there's no confirmation message or visual indicator of what happened. Did the doses get used? How many people were treated? The game silently returns to region selection.
- **Inconsistency in collapsed vs expanded threat display.** The non-selected disease collapses to hide region breakdown but keeps showing stats. This is fine, but the selected disease at 0% knowledge shows full region data while the collapsed view shows none -- the information reveal is driven by selection state, not knowledge level. This creates confusion about what knowledge actually gates.

## What I Wanted To Do But Couldn't

- **See infection details in region cards on the map.** The cards have empty lines that could show "Strain Alpha: 6.5M infected, 4.9M dead" but they only show this text inconsistently (North America had it, others did not despite having infections).
- **Get deployment confirmation.** After deploying medicine, I wanted to see "Deployed 10K doses of Antiviral-A to Asia. Treated 10K infected." -- some kind of outcome feedback.
- **See a visual indicator of active research.** When a research project is running, there's no persistent indicator anywhere that it's in progress. I have to re-open the Research panel and drill into Field Research or Bench Research to check on it. A small status line in the header or on the map would help.
- **See knowledge percentage for Strain Alpha in the Threats panel** after it passed 66%. The knowledge line simply disappeared, leaving me uncertain whether it was at 68%, 75%, or 100%.
- **See the health bars change color or fill level** as regions deteriorate. A region with 292M dead out of 4.7B population should look visually different from a healthy region.

## Ideas

1. **Make the health bars functional.** Show a multi-segment bar: green for healthy, yellow for infected, red for dead, blue for immune. This would make the map a genuine information display instead of decoration.

2. **Show infection summary inside every region card.** Even just "3 diseases, 83.6M infected, 25.1M dead" would make the map cards worth looking at. Currently only some regions show infection details, inconsistently.

3. **Use the empty bottom half of the screen.** Consider: event log, global statistics dashboard, resource income breakdown, research progress bars, or a news ticker. Currently the bottom half of both panels is blank.

4. **Standardize number formatting in deployment.** Change `4.7Bs 2.3Mi 7.6M checkmark` to use tabular columns like the Threats panel does: `Asia  4.7B susc  2.3M inf  7.6M imm` with proper spacing and labels.

5. **Show knowledge percentage always** in the Threats panel (even at high levels). Currently it disappears above 66%, which removes useful information. The player should always know how close they are to the next threshold.

6. **Add a research status indicator** to the header bar or hotkey bar. Something like "Field: Identify Strain Alpha (5/20)" would let players track research without re-opening the panel.

7. **Add deployment result feedback.** After deploying, briefly show the outcome in the panel: doses used, people treated/vaccinated, funds spent. Even a single status line would suffice.

8. **Consider a smaller, denser map layout.** The 2x3 grid of region cards with wide spacing takes a lot of vertical space while conveying little information. A compact table-style region list (like what the Threats panel already does) could live alongside the map, giving players both a geographic and a data view.

## Session Log

**Tick 0:** Game starts paused. Map takes full width with no panel. Immediate impression: the region cards are too big for the information they contain. Each card shows name, status label, population, a solid green bar, and maybe one line of text. 4 empty lines per card.

**Tick 0, Threats panel:** Two pathogens, both "Unknown." Pathogen #1 is selected and shows region data (Asia: 50K infected) even at 0% knowledge. Pathogen #2 is collapsed and shows nothing. The asymmetry between selected-view and collapsed-view is notable.

**Tick 0, Research panel:** Clean two-option layout. "Field Research" and "Bench Research" with one-line descriptions. Good use of Enter/Esc hints at the bottom.

**Tick 0, Medicines panel:** "No medicines available." Clean and clear. But the [Enter] Select hint is odd when there's nothing to select.

**Tick 0, Policy panel:** "Coming soon..." placeholder. Fine for now but it shouldn't be in the hotkey bar if it's non-functional.

**Tick 0, Help panel:** Minimal controls list. Adequate. Could mention the research/medicine pipeline to orient new players.

**Tick 0, Confirm Research (insufficient):** Shows cost, personnel, duration, then "Insufficient resources!" with only [Esc] Back. Good error state.

**Tick 10, started research:** Confirmed identify research with exactly 10 RP. The confirm screen was clear about costs.

**Tick 35, research complete:** Pathogen name revealed as "Strain Alpha." Back in Field Research list which still shows "Identify: Strain Alpha" -- meaning you can identify the same disease multiple times to increase knowledge. Not obvious from the UI that this is the intended flow.

**Tick 60, second identify done:** Knowledge at 68%. All stats now visible. Lethal and Recovery revealed. Good progressive disclosure.

**Tick 85, third identify done:** Knowledge line disappeared from Threats panel for Strain Alpha. No longer shows percentage. Confusing.

**Tick 85, Bench Research:** Now shows "Develop: Antiviral-A" and "Develop: Broad-Spectrum Antiviral." The progression from identify to develop works logically.

**Tick 130, medicine developed:** Antiviral-A available in Medicines panel. [UNTESTED] tag is clear warning. The 3-step deployment flow (select medicine -> select region -> select target) works smoothly.

**Tick 130, deployment flow:** Region selection screen uses cramped formatting: "4.7Bs 2.3Mi 7.6M checkmark." Hard to parse. Target selection screen is much clearer with proper labels and counts.

**Tick 130, deployed treatment:** Funds dropped $250 but no other feedback. Returned to region selection. Had to trust that it worked.

**Tick 330, 200 ticks later:** 215.8M dead. Only North America shows infection detail lines on the map. Europe at CRIT with millions dead shows nothing in its card. Health bars still all solid green.

**Tick 630, 300 more ticks:** 414.9M dead. Every region at CRIT or HIGH. Health bars unchanged. The dissonance between the CRIT labels and the cheerful green bars is jarring.
