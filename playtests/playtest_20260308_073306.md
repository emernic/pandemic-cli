# Playtest — 2026-03-08 07:33:06

Seed: 2024 (main game), 9999 (edge cases), 5555 (vaccination test) | Ticks played: ~330 across 3 sessions

## The Experience

I played three sessions: one strategic speedrun (seed 2024, won at tick 131), one edge-case probe (seed 9999), and one vaccination strategy test (seed 5555).

The main game felt satisfying. The research pipeline is clear once you understand it: identify (field) -> develop (bench) -> optionally trial (field) -> deploy. Running field and bench research in parallel is the key strategic insight. I won at tick 131, beating the 161 benchmark, by skipping clinical trials and deploying untested medicines. The adverse reaction system added real tension -- my first untested deployment killed 20K people, but my second and third were fine. That felt like a meaningful gamble.

The 2-round identification system (tested in #97) works cleanly: 2 x 50% = 100% exactly. After the first round, the disease name is revealed at 50%. After the second, all stats are visible at 100%. No float residue, no cap issues.

## What Stuck With Me

**Good:**
- The untested medicine warning dialog is excellent. Clear, scary, gives you real information ("25% chance of adverse effects -- 20% of doses will KILL instead of help") and lets you make an informed decision.
- The multi-disease BSA region list (#93) is a clear improvement. Showing susceptible/infected/immune per region for a multi-target medicine makes deployment decisions easy.
- The BSA deploy target screen for a multi-disease region (Asia) showing all 4 options (vaccinate/treat x 2 diseases) is well-organized and readable.
- Parallel field+bench research tracks create real strategic decisions about resource allocation.
- The research progress header ("Field: Identifying Unknown 25% | Bench: Antiviral-A 75%") gives at-a-glance status without opening any panel.
- Winning felt earned. The victory at tick 131 required good sequencing of research and deployment.

**Concerning:**
- Trying to start research with 0 RP silently closes all panels with no error message. At tick 0, I pressed R -> Enter -> Enter -> Enter and everything just vanished. A new player would be confused. There should be an "Insufficient RP" message in the status bar.
- You can start research and spend resources after the game is won (VICTORY state). I started a clinical trial at tick 131 post-victory and it consumed 15 RP and 5 personnel. This is harmless but feels like an oversight.
- The BSA region list shows combined susceptible counts across diseases (e.g., North America shows "1.0B sus" which is 500M x 2 diseases). This could confuse players who know NA's population is 500M. The summing makes mathematical sense for "how many vaccine targets exist" but doesn't match intuition.
- Status messages (like "Treated 73.2K in Asia with Broad-Spectrum Antiviral (-$500)") disappear after advancing ticks. This is fine for real-time play but in snapshot mode you can miss them if you chain --ticks with other commands.
- The Help panel doesn't mention Enter (for selecting items) or describe the research/medicine workflow at all. A new player gets zero guidance on the core gameplay loop.

## What I Wanted To Do But Couldn't

- **No way to cancel active research.** Once I accidentally started Antiviral-A development instead of BSA (misclicked), I was locked in for 40 ticks. I wanted to cancel and switch to BSA.
- **No game summary on victory.** When you win, you just see "All diseases eradicated! Humanity is saved." I wanted to see: total dead, ticks elapsed, money spent, medicines deployed, a score of some kind.
- **No way to see disease spread rate or projections.** The threats panel shows current numbers but I can't tell if the disease is accelerating or slowing. A trend indicator (arrows, sparklines, growth rate) would help decision-making.
- **No indication of which regions are connected for spread.** The map shows connection lines but I can't tell which regions are at risk of disease arriving next. When Strain Alpha was only in Asia, I had no way to prioritize vaccination of neighboring regions.
- **Vaccination felt pointless at scale.** 100K doses into a 500M population is a drop in the bucket. You'd need 5000 deployments at $200 each ($1M) to vaccinate North America against one disease. The vaccination strategy doesn't seem viable as a primary approach.

## Ideas

1. **Show an error message when research fails due to insufficient resources.** Even just "Not enough RP (need 10, have 0)" in the status bar would prevent confusion.
2. **Block actions during game over.** Or at least block resource-consuming actions (starting research, deploying medicine). Browsing panels is fine.
3. **Add a victory/defeat summary screen.** Show tick count, total dead, medicines deployed, money spent. This gives players something to optimize on replay.
4. **Expand the Help panel** to describe the research pipeline and mention Enter/Esc navigation. New players need to understand the identify -> develop -> trial -> deploy loop.
5. **Add research cancellation.** Even if you only get partial RP back (or none), being able to cancel a wrong research choice would reduce frustration.
6. **Clarify BSA region list susceptible counts.** Either show per-disease breakdowns, or show the actual population with a note like "2 diseases targetable."
