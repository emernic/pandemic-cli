# Playtest -- 2026-03-07

Seed: 999 | Ticks played: 120

## First Impressions

The initial screen is clean and easy to read. The top bar with PAUSED, tick count, and resources is immediately understandable. The region table with Pop/Alive/Infected/Dead/Risk is well-organized. The hotkey bar at the bottom is helpful. Starting paused is good -- it gives you a moment to orient.

The first thing I wanted to do was figure out what actions I could take. I checked every panel (T, R, M, P, ?) within the first minute. Three of four panels say "Coming soon..." which was deflating. It became immediately clear there is nothing to do except watch.

## What Worked

- **Header bar layout**: Tick count, resources, and infection totals at a glance. The human-readable number formatting (1.0K, 19.0M, 1.5B) scales well as the disaster grows.
- **Risk levels**: The OK/LOW/MOD/HIGH/CRIT progression is intuitive and gives quick visual triage without reading exact numbers.
- **Threats panel**: Shows disease stats and which regions are affected. The region list updates dynamically as the disease spreads, which is the one bit of information that actually changes in this panel.
- **Help screen**: Concise, accurate, lists all controls. No complaints.
- **Hotkey bar**: Always visible, changes "Resume" to "Pause" depending on state. Small but good detail.

## What Didn't Work

- **No player agency**: This is the critical issue. There are no actions to take. Resources (Funds, RP, Personnel) accumulate every tick but cannot be spent on anything. The game is currently a screensaver, not a game. I watched 1.5 billion people get infected and 305 million die with $1600 in the bank and 120 research points I could not use.
- **Cross-region spread is too slow and uneven**: The disease starts in Asia with 1K infected at tick 0. By tick 60, Asia has 1.6M infected but no other region has been touched. Spread finally reaches Europe/Africa/Oceania around tick 75-80, but even at tick 120, North America and South America are still at exactly zero. This makes the world map feel broken -- 2 of 6 regions never engage with the simulation at all during a 120-tick session.
- **No visual feedback for list navigation**: Pressing up/down in the main view does nothing visible. There is no highlight or selection indicator on the region list when no side panel is open.
- **Infected column shows "-0" for clean regions**: The dash-zero formatting (`-0`) is confusing. It looks like a rendering bug. A plain `0` or a dash `-` would be clearer.
- **Threats panel is too static**: It shows infectivity/lethality percentages and affected regions, but no per-region breakdown of infection counts, no growth rate, no trend indicator. The main table already shows the numbers; the threats panel should add analytical value beyond that.
- **Resources feel meaningless**: Funding goes up by $5/tick, RP by 1/tick, personnel stays at 50. None of these numbers connect to anything. There is no indication of what they will do or why I should care about them accumulating.

## Suggestions

1. **Add at least one spendable action** (highest impact): Even a single action like "Deploy Personnel to Region" that reduces infection rate would transform this from a screensaver into a game. Without player agency, playtesting the simulation is interesting for about 30 seconds.

2. **Fix cross-region spread balance**: The disease should reach all 6 regions within 40-50 ticks, not leave 2 regions untouched after 120. Consider making spread probability proportional to source infection count, or adding guaranteed spread once a region hits CRIT.

3. **Change "-0" display to "0" or "--"**: The current formatting reads as a negative number or a bug. Every clean region shows this and it is distracting.

4. **Add a trend indicator to the region table**: Something like an arrow or delta showing whether infections are growing, stable, or declining. Right now you can only tell by comparing snapshots mentally.

5. **Enrich the Threats panel**: Show per-region infection counts, total infected/dead for each disease, maybe a growth rate. Make it worth opening.

6. **Add an event log or ticker**: "Strain Alpha has spread to Europe" or "Asia deaths exceed 1M" -- some narrative thread to make the simulation feel alive beyond numbers changing.

7. **Give the player a reason to care about being paused vs. running**: Right now pause/unpause is irrelevant in snapshot mode and has no strategic consequence in real-time mode since there are no time-sensitive decisions.
