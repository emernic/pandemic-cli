# Playtest -- 2026-03-07 20:15

Seed: 42, 99, 77 | Ticks played: ~500 across 3 sessions

## The Experience

The medicine system is the first real thing to *do* in this game, and it immediately makes the game feel like a game rather than a screensaver. The three-step flow (pick medicine, pick region, pick target) is clear and logical. I found myself making real decisions: Do I treat the infected hotspot or vaccinate a clean region preemptively? The checkmark feedback after deployment is satisfying.

But the balance completely breaks the tension. In my first session (seed 42), I spent $100 on Antiviral-A to treat 1K infected in Asia at tick 0, and the disease was permanently eradicated. Game over in one click. Even with seed 99 where I let the disease grow to 1.6M before acting, two treatments ($400 total) wiped it out. There is no reason to ever feel pressure.

The most interesting moment was the vaccination experiment. I vaccinated Europe with 5M doses at tick 0 (seed 77) and let the disease run for 200 ticks. Without vaccination, Europe had 199M infected and 37.6M dead. With vaccination, Europe had only 3M infected and 471K dead. The vaccination genuinely reduced the susceptible pool enough to slow exponential growth. That is a real, satisfying mechanic -- it just needs the numbers tuned so it requires more investment to achieve.

## What Stuck With Me

**Good:**
- The three-step deployment flow felt natural and not tedious. Medicine -> Region -> Target is intuitive.
- The deploy panel showing "741.5Ms 3.0Mi 5.0M checkmark" gives you real information to decide where to act next.
- Returning to SelectRegion after deployment enables rapid multi-region deployment. Smart design choice.
- The "Cost: $100  Funding: $1000" display at the target step prevents guesswork.
- Vaccination genuinely affects disease spread dynamics, not just a cosmetic number change.

**Bad:**
- The "-0" display for regions with no infection data is visually distracting. Should just be "0" or blank.
- No feedback when deployment fails (insufficient funds or 0 targets). It silently bounces back to the region list. A message like "Insufficient funds" or "No valid targets" would help.
- The Threats panel shows a region as having Strain Alpha if you vaccinated there, even before the disease arrives. This is confusing -- vaccination should not make it look like the disease is present.
- Both medicines target "Strain Alpha" with no distinction other than cost/doses. There is no reason to use Antiviral-A when Broad-Spectrum is 5x the doses for 3x the cost.

## What I Wanted To Do But Couldn't

- **See immune/vaccinated counts in the main World Status.** The main region table shows Pop, Alive, Infected, Dead -- but not Immune. After vaccinating, I have no way to see the effect without going back into the deploy panel. An "Immune" column would complete the picture.
- **Get feedback on deployment failure.** When I tried treating a region with 0 infected, or deploying with $0 funds, nothing happened and there was no message explaining why. I was momentarily confused.
- **Compare regions more easily.** When deciding where to vaccinate, I wanted to see infection rate/growth, not just current counts. A region with 2M infected but slowing growth is different from one with 2M infected and accelerating.
- **Queue up multiple deployments.** With 6 regions to vaccinate and each requiring navigate-select-confirm, I wished I could deploy to multiple regions in one step. The rapid re-deployment (returning to region selection) partially addresses this, but it still requires many keypresses.

## Ideas

1. **Balance: Make the disease fight back.** Treatment should not permanently cure a region. Options: disease re-emerges from recovered carriers, cross-region reinfection, mutation. Currently one $100 treatment at tick 0 ends the game.
2. **Balance: Scale medicine costs or doses.** 1M doses for $100 against 1K infected is absurdly overpowered. Either start the disease at higher numbers, make medicines more expensive, give fewer doses per deployment, or add a cooldown between deployments.
3. **Add an "Immune" column to the world status table.** Players need to see their vaccination progress reflected in the main view.
4. **Show deployment failure messages.** Flash "Insufficient funds!" or "No targets in this region" for 1-2 seconds after a failed deployment instead of silently bouncing back.
5. **Fix the "-0" display.** Show "0" or "--" for regions with no infection data.
6. **Differentiate the two medicines.** Give Antiviral-A a unique advantage: faster deployment, works on multiple strains, cheaper per-dose for small outbreaks. Currently Broad-Spectrum strictly dominates on cost-per-dose ($60/M vs $100/M).
7. **Consider a deployment cooldown or per-region limit.** Prevent the player from spam-vaccinating every region in tick 0. Perhaps medicines take time to manufacture or deploy.
