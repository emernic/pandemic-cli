# Playtest -- 2026-03-07 18:05

Seed: 42 | Ticks played: ~815

## The Experience

I watched humanity die. That's basically the whole story.

The game opens cleanly -- paused, one disease in Asia, 1K infected. You orient quickly: six regions, a status bar with resources, hotkeys for panels along the bottom. Good first impression. I unpaused and started exploring the panels.

The Threats panel is useful. It told me Strain Alpha has 15% infectivity and 2% lethality, and which regions it had reached. The other three panels (Research, Medicines, Policy) all say "Coming soon." That's the moment the experience breaks: you realize there's nothing to do. You're watching a screensaver of human extinction.

The disease tore through Asia first -- exponential growth hit hard around tick 50-100, going from thousands to hundreds of millions of infected. It spread to Europe, Africa, and Oceania next, with the Americas holding out until around tick 200. By tick 315, 6.2 billion were dead. By tick 515, 7.8 billion. The simulation eventually resolved itself: the disease burned through its host population and the infection rate dropped, leaving small pockets of survivors (2.1M in Asia, 20K in Oceania).

There was no game over screen. No win or loss condition. The simulation just kept ticking, resources kept accumulating uselessly ($5075, 815 RP at the end), and the death toll stayed frozen because there was almost nobody left to die.

## What Stuck With Me

**The helplessness is the dominant feeling.** Resources pile up (funding, research points, personnel) and you have zero ways to spend them. The game teaches you to care about those numbers in the header, then makes them meaningless.

**The spread model is satisfying to watch.** The logistic growth curve feels right -- exponential early, plateau, then decline as the population is consumed. The cross-region spread timing felt plausible too. The Americas holding out longer than Oceania was an interesting asymmetry.

**The "-0" display for zero infections is distracting.** Every uninfected region shows "-0" instead of "0" or "-" or blank. It reads like a formatting bug.

**Risk levels going DOWN after most people are dead is darkly funny but misleading.** At tick 815, Oceania shows "LOW" risk with 20K survivors out of 45M. That's not low risk, that's post-apocalyptic. The risk calculation seems to be based on current infection rate rather than cumulative devastation.

**No endgame.** The game needs to tell you when it's over. Whether that's "humanity fell" or "the outbreak was contained" or something in between.

## What I Wanted To Do But Couldn't

- **Spend resources.** I wanted to fund research, deploy medicines, close borders, quarantine regions -- anything. The building blocks are clearly here (resources accumulate, the panel hotkeys exist) but there's nothing behind them yet.

- **See a timeline or graph.** I kept wanting a sense of trajectory -- is the infection accelerating or decelerating? The numbers change between my checks but I have no sense of the rate of change.

- **Get notifications.** "Strain Alpha has reached Europe" should be an event I see, not something I notice by checking the threats panel or reading the main table carefully.

- **Click on a region** (or select it with arrow keys) to see more detail about what's happening there.

- **See the end.** A game over screen, a score, a summary -- anything to give closure.

## Ideas

1. **Implement one action first, not all four panels.** Even a simple "deploy quarantine to region X, costs $500, reduces spread by 50%" would transform this from a screensaver into a game. Pick the simplest possible intervention and ship it.

2. **Add win/loss conditions.** Loss: total infected exceeds some threshold, or total dead exceeds some fraction of world population. Win: all infections reach zero. Display a clear endgame screen.

3. **Add an event log.** A scrolling text area at the bottom: "Tick 87: Strain Alpha detected in Europe." "Tick 150: Asia death toll exceeds 1 billion." This would give the simulation narrative texture even before player actions exist.

4. **Fix the "-0" display.** Show "0" or "--" or leave blank for regions with zero infected/dead.

5. **Rethink risk levels.** A region with 99% of its population dead should not show "LOW" risk. Consider factoring in cumulative death rate, not just current infection velocity.

6. **Show rate of change.** Even a simple up/down arrow next to the infected count (increasing vs decreasing) would add a lot of information density.
