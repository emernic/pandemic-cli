# Playtest -- 2026-03-08 05:03

Seed: 8308 | Ticks played: ~265

## The Experience

Started with two diseases: one large outbreak in Asia (50K, later identified as Strain Alpha with 15% infection rate) and a small one in South America (500, later identified as Strain Beta with 8% infection rate). I pursued an aggressive research-then-vaccinate strategy, focusing all early research on identifying and developing medicine for the larger threat.

The research pipeline worked smoothly: three identification rounds to reach 100% knowledge (tedious but functional), then bench research to develop Antiviral-A, then a clinical trial. By the time I had a tested medicine (tick 165), Asia already had 6.4M infected and 4.3M dead. The 10K dose deployments were laughably inadequate against millions of infections.

I tried a preventive vaccination strategy, vaccinating Europe, North America, and Africa before Strain Alpha reached them. The 10K doses per deployment meant vaccinating roughly 0.001% of a region's population. The disease eventually arrived in all regions regardless. The vaccinations were too small to create any meaningful barrier.

## What Stuck With Me

**Untested vaccine killed 2,000 people in Africa.** I deployed the untested Antiviral-A to three regions. North America and Europe got 10K vaccinations with no adverse effects, but Africa got only 8K immune and 2K dead. The adverse effect mechanic (25% chance, 20% of doses become lethal) applies even to vaccination of healthy people. This feels deeply wrong -- adverse effects from treating sick people makes sense, but a vaccine killing 20% of healthy recipients is bizarre. The game doesn't warn you about this before deployment (known issue #54), and the outcome of healthy people dying from a preventive vaccine feels like a bug in the game design rather than a feature.

**Personnel header is misleading.** The header always shows "Personnel: 50" even when 5-15 are assigned to active research projects. The confirmation screen correctly shows "5 (50 available)" but the top bar never changes. You can only see actual availability when starting a new project. This should show "Personnel: 45/50" or similar.

**Cross-region spread timing.** Strain Alpha (15% infection) first spread beyond Asia around tick 65-70, roughly 35 seconds of real time. Strain Beta (8% infection) didn't spread beyond South America until somewhere between tick 110-165. The different spread rates feel meaningful and tied to infection rate, which is good.

**Vaccination creates a disease entry in the region.** When I vaccinated North America (which had no Strain Alpha), the region card changed from "No infections" to showing "Strain Alpha / Inf 0". This is technically accurate (the disease tracking now exists there) but visually alarming -- a region that was clean suddenly shows a disease name on its card. A player might think they accidentally introduced the disease.

**Threats panel totals are mathematically correct.** I verified cross-region aggregation: Europe shows Inf 87, Dead 43 on the map card. Strain Alpha contributes 62 infected + 40 dead, Strain Beta contributes 25 infected + 3 dead. 62+25=87, 40+3=43. Totals check out perfectly.

**The map connection label "left-right-arrow Oceania" is confusing.** There's text that reads "left-right-arrow Oceania" (with a Unicode arrow) between the top and bottom rows of region cards, on the left side. I think it's indicating a connection between North America and Oceania (Pacific crossing), but it's not visually clear what it means. The horizontal --- and diagonal / connection lines between adjacent cards are clear, but this wrap-around connection indicator is cryptic.

## What I Wanted To Do But Couldn't

1. **Increase medicine production.** With 10K doses against millions of infections, I wanted to invest in scaling up production. There's no way to increase the dose count of a medicine -- you just deploy the same 10K over and over at $250 each.

2. **See personnel in use.** When I had two research projects running (field + bench), I wanted to see "Personnel: 35/50" in the header so I could plan resource allocation without opening the research panel.

3. **Queue research.** After the first identification completed, I wanted the next one to start automatically. Instead I had to manually navigate back to the research panel and start each new project.

4. **Understand the connections.** I wanted a legend or tooltip explaining which regions are connected to which. The lines on the map imply connections but the wrap-around "left-right-arrow Oceania" label is not self-explanatory.

5. **Deploy medicine in bulk.** When Asia has 73M infected and I can only treat 10K at a time for $250, the deploy-one-batch-at-a-time workflow feels futile. I wanted a "deploy all remaining funds" or "deploy N batches" option.

## Ideas

1. **Fix adverse effects for vaccination.** Untested medicine adverse effects should only apply when treating infected patients, not when vaccinating healthy people. A vaccine killing 20% of healthy recipients doesn't model any real-world phenomenon and produces confusing results (8K immune + 2K dead from vaccinating a disease-free region).

2. **Show personnel in use in the header.** Change "Personnel: 50" to "Personnel: 35/50" when research projects are consuming personnel. This is a small UI change with big clarity benefit.

3. **Clarify the vaccination-creates-disease-entry behavior.** When vaccinating a disease-free region, either (a) don't show the disease name on the region card if infected=0, or (b) show it differently, like "Strain Alpha (vaccinated)" instead of "Strain Alpha / Inf 0". The current behavior makes it look like you brought the disease there.

4. **Make the Oceania wrap-around connection visually clearer.** The "left-right-arrow Oceania" text is cryptic. Consider a different visual treatment, or add a small connection legend to the map.
