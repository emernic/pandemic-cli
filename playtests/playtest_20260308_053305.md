# Playtest -- 2026-03-08 05:33:05

Seed: 8308 | Ticks played: ~500

## The Experience

I started at tick 0 with two unknown pathogens: one in Asia (50K infected) and one in South America (500 infected). The first ~90 ticks were spent on research -- identifying both pathogens through repeated field research rounds, each costing 10 RP and taking 20 ticks. By tick 90 I had Strain Alpha at 100% knowledge and Strain Beta at 68%.

I developed Broad-Spectrum Antiviral first (bench research, 30 RP, 40 ticks) because it targets both diseases. At tick 130 I deployed it for the first time -- treating 50K Strain Alpha infected in Asia (2.3M infected at the time) and then 50K Strain Beta infected in South America (153.2K infected). These were drops in the bucket. Each deployment cost $600 and funds only accumulate at $5/tick, so I was constantly broke.

By tick 300, 174.7M were infected and 137.9M dead. My three total deployments of 50K doses each had treated 150K people total -- against hundreds of millions infected. The diseases burned through populations via natural herd immunity. By tick 500, infections had dropped to 6.9M but 343M were dead. The diseases essentially resolved themselves through mass death and natural recovery, not through my interventions.

## What Stuck With Me

**The Broad-Spectrum Antiviral deployment flow is well-designed.** When you select it for a region, you get four clear options: vaccinate susceptible or treat infected, for each of the two diseases it targets. The labels clearly show counts (e.g., "2.3M infected | 50.0K doses"). You choose which disease to target per deployment -- it does not treat both simultaneously.

**The dose numbers are precisely tracked.** Deploying 50K doses to treat Strain Beta in South America (153.2K infected) reduced infected by exactly 50K (to 103.2K) and increased immune by exactly 50K (from 101.9K to 151.9K). Treatment converts infected to immune 1:1 with no waste.

**Deploying to a region with only one of the BSA's target diseases works fine.** You just see 0 infected for the other disease and can still choose to vaccinate susceptible for either.

**Bench research shows nothing when all medicines are developed.** Entering bench research at that point just closes the panel with no message. There's no "No projects available" feedback.

**Insufficient funds deployment fails silently.** At $0 funds I tried to deploy the $600 BSA. It appeared to accept my region selection but then just returned to the region selection screen with no error message. No visual or textual feedback that I lack funds.

**The untested medicine deployed without a warning or adverse effect.** BSA was marked [UNTESTED] throughout the session. I deployed it three times with no adverse effects triggering (25% chance per deployment). The [UNTESTED] label was visible in the medicines list but not on the deployment confirmation screen.

**The BSA clinical trial completed but the medicine still shows [UNTESTED].** I ran "Trial: Broad-Spectrum Antiviral vs Strain Alpha" (completed around tick 235), but at tick 500 the medicine still displays [UNTESTED]. This may be because the BSA needs separate trials per disease -- one for Alpha and one for Beta -- and I only completed the Alpha trial. But the UI gives no indication that the medicine is "partially tested" or that another trial is needed.

**Q key does not produce a quit dialog in snapshot mode.** Pressing Q from any screen just closes the current panel or does nothing. The hotkey bar shows "[Q] Quit" but the key has no special quit behavior.

## What I Wanted To Do But Couldn't

**See the deployment effect.** After deploying medicine, there's no confirmation message, no summary of what happened (e.g., "Treated 50,000 infected in Asia"). You have to compare numbers before and after to know it worked.

**Scale up deployments.** 50K doses against millions of infected felt futile. I wanted to spend my accumulated 275+ RP on something -- more doses, bigger batches, faster production -- but there's nothing to spend RP on once research is done.

**See total population breakdown at a glance.** The threats panel shows infected/immune/dead per region, but susceptible population is never displayed. You have to mentally subtract. A region with 4.7B population, 28M infected, 1.3B immune, 268M dead -- how many are still susceptible? I had to calculate it myself.

**Understand the region selection display for BSA.** The deployment region list shows something like `Asia 4.7Bs 5.4Mi 18.5M checkmark 4.7Bs 0i` -- two columns of data crammed into one line, one per disease. The `s` for susceptible, `i` for infected, checkmark for immune abbreviations are very dense and hard to parse at speed.

**Navigate the map meaningfully.** Left/right arrows move a selection indicator between regions, but it doesn't seem to do anything useful. There's no detail view for a selected region. The indicator (a `<->` label) appears inconsistently.

## Ideas

**Add a "No projects available" message for empty bench/field research.** When all medicines are developed, bench research should show an empty state message rather than silently closing the panel.

**Add an "Insufficient funds" error message.** When deployment fails due to cost, show a clear message like "Cannot deploy: need $600, have $0."

**Show a deployment result summary.** After deploying, briefly display what happened: "Deployed 50,000 doses to Asia. Treated 50,000 Strain Alpha infected. 0 doses wasted." This would also clarify the dose waste question.

**Show susceptible count in the threats panel.** Adding a "Susceptible" column to the region breakdown would save mental math and make the epidemic trajectory clearer.

**Improve the BSA region selection display.** Label each disease column clearly. Instead of `Asia 4.7Bs 5.4Mi 18.5M checkmark 4.7Bs 0i`, show something like:
```
Asia
  Alpha: 4.7B susceptible, 5.4M infected, 18.5M immune
  Beta:  4.7B susceptible, 0 infected
```

**Clarify partial clinical trial status.** If BSA needs trials for each disease separately, show that progress: "Tested against: Strain Alpha. Untested against: Strain Beta."
