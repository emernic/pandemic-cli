# Playtest -- 2026-03-08 09:03

Seed: 777 (speed run), 99 (passive run), 1/100/12345 (seed comparison) | Ticks played: ~1884 (passive) + ~161 (speed run) + misc

## The Experience

I ran two distinct sessions: a passive "do nothing" run to find the natural defeat timeline, and an aggressive speed run to find the fastest possible victory. Along the way I verified two recent bug fixes (#113 and #110) and tested seed variation.

The passive run was surprisingly slow to kill humanity. Defeat came at tick 1884 (about 15 minutes of real time at 500ms/tick) with 784.2M dead (10% threshold). The diseases spread methodically but not explosively -- the exponential curve is very gradual in the early hundreds of ticks, only becoming threatening after tick 400-500. This means a player who starts intervening even at tick 200 still has a fighting chance.

The speed run was satisfying. Victory at tick 161 with only 86.1K dead. The critical path was: identify Strain Alpha twice (ticks 10-50), start bench research on Antiviral-A (ticks 50-90), identify Strain Beta in parallel (ticks 60-80), treat Asia with Antiviral-A (tick 90), start bench research on BSA (ticks 110-150), treat remaining infections with BSA (tick 160). The parallel field + bench research slots are the key optimization.

## What Stuck With Me

**The adverse reaction mechanic hit hard.** On my second Antiviral-A deployment to Asia, I got "ADVERSE REACTION: 11.8K died". I knew the risk (25% chance, clearly warned), but seeing it happen still stung. It creates real tension around whether to run the clinical trial or save time deploying untested.

**The "No infected population" rejection for #113 works perfectly.** When I tried treating 0 Strain Alpha infected in North America and South America, the game blocked it with a clear message and charged nothing. No ghost disease entries appeared on the region cards afterward.

**#110 is fixed.** After both victory and defeat, Research and Medicines panels show only "[Esc] Close" -- no "[Enter] Select" hints that would mislead players into thinking they can still interact.

**Seeds produce identical initial conditions.** Seeds 1, 100, and 12345 all start with exactly the same setup: 50K infected in Asia, 500 in South America, $1000 funds, 0 RP, 50 personnel. The seed only affects RNG during simulation (spread patterns, adverse reaction rolls), not initial disease placement or parameters.

**Medicine doses appear unlimited.** Antiviral-A showed "100.0K doses" before AND after deployment. Each deployment seems to give a fresh batch. This made the speed run trivial -- I just kept deploying the same medicine repeatedly without worrying about supply.

## What I Wanted To Do But Couldn't

**No way to cancel a research project.** Once I committed to developing BSA, I had to wait the full 40 ticks. If I'd made a strategic mistake, there was no way to abort and redirect resources.

**Can't fast-forward while waiting for research.** In the speed run, much of my time was spent waiting for research to finish. A "skip to next event" button would help pacing in real-time play.

**No feedback on natural recovery.** Diseases seem to recover naturally (Oceania went from Inf 7 to lower numbers between checks), but there's no indication of recovery rate or when a disease might burn out on its own.

**Policy panel is still placeholder.** "Coming soon..." text has been there since early playtests.

## Ideas

1. **Seed should vary initial conditions.** Different seeds should randomize which regions start infected, disease parameters, and starting resources. This is the single biggest gap in replayability right now.

2. **Add a score/rating system.** Victory at tick 161 with 86K dead felt good, but there's no grading. "S rank: under 50K dead" or similar would add replay motivation.

3. **Consider making medicine doses finite per game.** If Antiviral-A only had 100K doses total (not per deployment), players would face meaningful trade-offs about where to deploy. Currently you can spam deployments with infinite restocking.

4. **Show elapsed real-time on the victory/defeat screen.** "Duration: 161 ticks" is less meaningful than "Duration: 1 minute 20 seconds".

5. **Strain Beta was barely a threat.** In the speed run, Strain Beta only reached 19K infected in South America before I wiped it out. It never spread beyond its starting region in meaningful numbers. Consider making it more aggressive or starting it in a more connected region.
